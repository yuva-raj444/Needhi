JUVENILE JUSTICE (CARE AND PROTECTION OF CHILDREN) ACT, 2015

SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS
(12) "Child" means a person who has not completed eighteen years of age.
(13) "Child in conflict with law" means a child who is alleged or found to have committed an offence.
(14) "Child in need of care and protection" means a child who:
(i) is found without any home or settled place of abode and without any ostensible means of subsistence;
(ii) is found working in contravention of labour laws;
(iii) lives with a person who has threatened to kill, injure or exploit the child;
(iv) is found begging;
(v) is being or is likely to be abused, tortured or exploited;
(vi) has a parent or guardian who is incapable of caring for the child;
(vii) is missing or run away;
(viii) has been or is being married below the prescribed age.

SECTION 3 — GENERAL PRINCIPLES OF CARE AND PROTECTION
Every child shall be treated in accordance with principles of:
(i) Presumption of innocence
(ii) Dignity
(iii) Participation
(iv) Best interest of the child
(v) Family responsibility
(vi) Safety
(vii) Non-stigmatising semantics, non-adversarial proceedings
(viii) Repatriation and restoration
(ix) Fresh start
(x) Diversion

SECTION 15 — PRELIMINARY ASSESSMENT INTO HEINOUS OFFENCES BY JUVENILE JUSTICE BOARD
(1) In case of a heinous offence alleged to have been committed by a child who has completed or is above the age of sixteen years, the Board shall conduct a preliminary assessment to determine:
(a) whether the child has the mental and physical capacity to commit such offence;
(b) whether the child had the ability to understand the consequences;
(c) the circumstances in which the offence was committed.
(2) If the Board finds that the child should be tried as an adult, the Board may transfer the case to the Children's Court.

SECTION 18 — ORDERS THAT MAY BE PASSED BY THE BOARD
(1) Where the Board is satisfied that the child has committed an offence, it may:
(a) allow the child to go home after advice or admonition;
(b) direct the child to participate in group counselling;
(c) order the child to perform community service;
(d) order the child or parents to pay fine;
(e) direct the child to be released on probation;
(f) direct the child to be sent to a special home for a period not exceeding 3 years.

SECTION 56 — ADOPTION
(1) Adoption shall be resorted to for ensuring the right to family for orphan, abandoned and surrendered children.
(2) Adoption shall be regulated by the Central Adoption Resource Authority (CARA).

SECTION 57 — ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA FOR PROSPECTIVE ADOPTIVE PARENTS
(1) The prospective adoptive parent(s) shall be physically fit, financially sound, mentally alert and highly motivated to adopt a child.
(2) Single parents: May adopt; female can adopt any child; single male cannot adopt a girl child.
(3) Couple must be married for at least 2 years and have a stable marriage.
(4) Age criteria: If child is up to 4 years, parents should be not more than 25 years older; 4-8 years, not more than 30 years older; 8-18 years, not more than 35 years older.

PROHIBITION OF CHILD MARRIAGE ACT, 2006

SECTION 2 — DEFINITION OF CHILD
"Child" means a person who, if male, has not completed twenty-one years of age, and if female, has not completed eighteen years of age.

SECTION 3 — CHILD MARRIAGE VOIDABLE AT THE OPTION OF CONTRACTING PARTY BEING A CHILD
(1) Every child marriage shall be voidable at the option of the contracting party who was a child at the time of the marriage.
(2) A petition for annulling a child marriage may be filed at any time but before the child completes two years of attaining majority (i.e., before age 20 for girls, before age 23 for boys).

SECTION 9 — PUNISHMENT FOR MALE ADULT MARRYING A CHILD
Any male adult who contracts a child marriage shall be punishable with rigorous imprisonment up to two years, or fine up to one lakh rupees, or both.

SECTION 10 — PUNISHMENT FOR SOLEMNIZING A CHILD MARRIAGE
Whoever performs, conducts, directs, or abets a child marriage shall be punishable with rigorous imprisonment up to two years, or fine up to one lakh rupees, or both.

SECTION 11 — PUNISHMENT FOR PROMOTING OR PERMITTING CHILD MARRIAGE
(1) Any person having charge of a child who promotes or permits the marriage of such child shall be punishable with rigorous imprisonment up to two years, or fine up to one lakh rupees, or both.
(2) This includes parents, guardians, or any person responsible for the child.

DOWRY PROHIBITION ACT, 1961

SECTION 2 — DEFINITION OF DOWRY
"Dowry" means any property or valuable security given or agreed to be given either directly or indirectly:
(a) by one party to a marriage to the other party to the marriage; or
(b) by the parents of either party to a marriage or by any other person, to either party to the marriage or to any other person, at or before or any time after the marriage in connection with the marriage.
It does not include dower or mahr in the case of persons to whom the Muslim Personal Law applies.

SECTION 3 — PENALTY FOR GIVING OR TAKING DOWRY
If any person gives or takes or abets the giving or taking of dowry, he shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term not less than five years and fine not less than Rs 15,000 or the amount of the value of such dowry, whichever is more.

SECTION 4 — PENALTY FOR DEMANDING DOWRY
If any person demands, directly or indirectly, from the parents or other relatives of a bride or bridegroom any dowry, he shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term not less than six months, extendable to two years, and fine up to Rs 10,000.

SECTION 6 — DOWRY TO BE FOR BENEFIT OF WIFE OR HEIRS
If any dowry is received by any person other than the woman, it shall be transferred to the woman within three months from the date of marriage or date of receipt, whichever is later.

SEXUAL HARASSMENT OF WOMEN AT WORKPLACE (PREVENTION, PROHIBITION AND REDRESSAL) ACT, 2013

SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS
(n) "Sexual harassment" includes:
(i) Physical contact and advances;
(ii) A demand or request for sexual favours;
(iii) Making sexually coloured remarks;
(iv) Showing pornography;
(v) Any other unwelcome physical, verbal or non-verbal conduct of sexual nature.

SECTION 4 — CONSTITUTION OF INTERNAL COMPLAINTS COMMITTEE
Every employer of a workplace shall constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) at each office or branch with 10 or more employees.
Composition:
(a) Presiding Officer — a woman employed at a senior level;
(b) Not less than two members from amongst employees;
(c) One member from a NGO or association committed to women's cause or a person familiar with issues of sexual harassment.
(d) At least 1/2 of the total members shall be women.

SECTION 7 — LOCAL COMPLAINTS COMMITTEE
In every district, a Local Complaints Committee shall be constituted for receiving complaints from employees of establishments having less than 10 workers or from domestic workers.

SECTION 9 — COMPLAINT
(1) Any aggrieved woman may make, in writing, a complaint of sexual harassment to the ICC or LCC within a period of three months from the date of incident.
(2) If the aggrieved woman is unable to make a complaint in writing, the Presiding Officer shall render all reasonable assistance for making the complaint.

SECTION 11 — INQUIRY
(1) The ICC or LCC shall make an inquiry into the complaint within 90 days.
(2) During pendency, the ICC may recommend:
(a) transfer of the aggrieved woman or the respondent;
(b) grant leave to the aggrieved woman up to 3 months;
(c) grant such other relief.

SECTION 13 — ACTION ON INQUIRY
(1) If the ICC/LCC concludes that the allegation has been proved:
(a) Recommend disciplinary action against the respondent;
(b) Recommend deduction from the respondent's salary for payment of compensation to the aggrieved woman;
(c) Recommend payment of compensation (considering mental trauma, loss of career opportunity, medical expenses, income and financial status of the respondent).

SECTION 14 — FALSE OR MALICIOUS COMPLAINT
If the ICC/LCC concludes that the allegation was false, malicious, or made with intent to defame — action may be recommended against the complainant. However, mere inability to substantiate a complaint shall NOT attract action.

SECTION 26 — EMPLOYER'S DUTY
Every employer shall:
(a) Provide a safe working environment;
(b) Display the penal consequences of sexual harassment and the order of ICC;
(c) Organize workshops and awareness programs;
(d) Provide assistance in filing complaints;
(e) Include sexual harassment provisions in the standing orders.

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE BY EMPLOYER (Section 26):
First offence: Fine up to Rs 50,000.
Second offence: Double the fine and/or cancellation of business licence.

WHISTLE BLOWERS PROTECTION ACT, 2014

PURPOSE: To protect persons who make disclosures relating to acts of corruption, misuse of power, or criminal offence by public servants.

Any person may make a public interest disclosure before the Central or State Vigilance Commission relating to an act of corruption, wilful misuse of power, wilful misuse of discretion, or commission of a criminal offence by a public servant.

The identity of the complainant shall be kept secret. Any person who has disclosed the identity of the complainant shall be punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs 50,000.
