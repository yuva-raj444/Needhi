TRANSFER OF PROPERTY ACT, 1882 - LANDLORD & TENANT RIGHTS

SECTION 107 — DEVELOPMENT AND MAINTENANCE CHARGES
The lessor and lessee, in the absence of agreement to the contrary, are responsible for maintenance of the property as per the lease deed.

SECTION 108 — LESSOR'S DUTY
(1) The lessor must allow the lessee to enjoy the property during the lease.
(2) The lessor must make repairs necessary to keep the property in fit condition.

SECTION 115 — LESSEE'S DUTY
(1) The lessee must pay the rent as agreed.
(2) The lessee must not commit waste or permanent injury to the property.
(3) The lessee must maintain the property as per the lease terms.

SECTION 116 — LESSOR'S RIGHT TO DISTRAIN
(1) The lessor can distrain goods of the lessee for non-payment of rent.

DEPOSIT AND REFUND - IMPORTANT
1. Security deposit is held in trust for the tenant
2. Landlord must return deposit within 30 days of lease termination
3. Deductions can only be made for actual damages or unpaid rent
4. Interest on deposit should be paid as per state law (usually 8-12% per annum)
5. Landlord cannot arbitrarily withhold security deposit

TENANT RIGHTS
1. Right to quiet enjoyment of the property
2. Right to get repairs done within reasonable time
3. Right to get full security deposit back (with interest)
4. Right to privacy - landlord cannot enter without notice
5. Protection against arbitrary eviction

LANDLORD DUTIES
1. Maintain the property in habitable condition
2. Make necessary repairs
3. Ensure water and electrical connections
4. Return security deposit with interest within 30 days
5. Follow due legal process for eviction

EVICTION PROCESS
Landlord cannot evict without following proper legal procedure:
1. Notice must be given (usually 30-60 days)
2. Reason must be valid (non-payment, breach, end of lease)
3. Court order is mandatory
4. Tenant has right to defend in court
