SCHEDULED CASTES AND SCHEDULED TRIBES (PREVENTION OF ATROCITIES) ACT, 1989
(As amended in 2015 and 2018)

PURPOSE: To prevent atrocities against members of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes and to provide for Special Courts and rehabilitation.

SECTION 3 — OFFENCES OF ATROCITIES
(1) Whoever, not being a member of a Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe, commits any of the following offences against a person belonging to SC/ST:
(a) forces a member of SC/ST to eat or drink any inedible or obnoxious substance;
(b) dumps excreta, sewage, carcasses or any other obnoxious substance in premises or neighbourhood of a member of SC/ST;
(c) forcibly removes clothes from a member of SC/ST, parades them naked or with painted face or body;
(d) wrongfully occupies or cultivates any land owned by or allotted to a member of SC/ST;
(e) wrongfully dispossesses a member of SC/ST from his land or premises;
(f) compels or entices a member of SC/ST to do begar (forced labour) or bonded labour;
(g) forces a member of SC/ST to leave his house, village, or other place of residence;
(h) prevents a member of SC/ST from using any public place or water source;
(i) forces or intimidates a member of SC/ST to vote or not vote for any candidate;
(j) institutes false, malicious or vexatious criminal proceedings against a member of SC/ST;
(k) gives false or frivolous information to any public servant and thereby causes such public servant to take action against a member of SC/ST;
(l) intentionally insults or intimidates a member of SC/ST in any place within public view;
(m) assaults or uses force against a member of SC/ST woman with intent to dishonour her;
(n) being in a position to dominate the will of a woman belonging to SC/ST, uses that position to exploit her sexually;
(o) corrupts or fouls the water of any spring, reservoir or any other source ordinarily used by SC/ST members;
(p) denies a member of SC/ST any customary right of passage to a place of public resort;
(q) forces or causes a member of SC/ST to leave his house, village, or residence;
(r) obstructs or prevents a member of SC/ST from performing duties as a Panchayat member;
(s) destroys, damages or defiles any object generally known to be held sacred by members of SC/ST.

SECTION 3(1)(r) — COMMITS ANY OFFENCE UNDER IPC/BNS PUNISHABLE WITH 10+ YEARS
If any offence under the IPC/BNS punishable with imprisonment of 10 years or more is committed against a member of SC/ST — imprisonment for life and fine.

SECTION 3(2) — ADDITIONAL OFFENCES
(v) Commits any offence under the IPC/BNS punishable with imprisonment of ten years or more against a person or property on the ground that such person is a member of a Scheduled Caste or a Scheduled Tribe.
(va) Sexual exploitation of a woman belonging to SC/ST.
(vii) Being a public servant, not registering an FIR — imprisonment of 6 months to 1 year.
(viii) Fabricating false evidence or giving false information to a public servant — imprisonment of 6 months to 3 years.

PUNISHMENT:
Most offences: Imprisonment not less than 6 months, extendable to 5 years, and fine.
Serious offences (murder, rape, etc.): As per the substantive offence, with minimum mandatory sentences.
Offences causing death: Death or imprisonment for life.

SECTION 4 — PUNISHMENT FOR NEGLECT OF DUTIES BY PUBLIC SERVANT
Any non-SC/ST public servant who willfully neglects his duties required to be performed under this Act — imprisonment for 6 months to 1 year.

SECTION 14 — SPECIAL COURTS
The State Government shall establish a Special Court in each district for speedy trial of offences under this Act.

SECTION 15A — RIGHTS OF VICTIMS AND WITNESSES (Added by 2015 Amendment)
(1) The victim or his dependent shall have right to reasonable, accurate, and timely notice of any court proceeding.
(2) The victim and witnesses shall be given protection.
(3) The victim shall be treated with fairness, respect and dignity.

SECTION 18 — NO ANTICIPATORY BAIL
Nothing in Section 438 CrPC (now Section 482 BNSS) shall apply in relation to any case involving the arrest of any person on an accusation of having committed an offence under this Act. (However, the Supreme Court in Subhash Kashinath Mahajan vs State of Maharashtra, 2018 had diluted this, which was later reversed by the SC/ST Amendment Act, 2018.)

SECTION 18A — NO PRELIMINARY INQUIRY FOR REGISTRATION OF FIR (Added 2018)
(a) No preliminary inquiry shall be required for registration of FIR.
(b) The investigating officer shall not require approval for the arrest of the accused.
(c) The provisions of Section 438 CrPC shall not apply to a case under this Act.

SECTION 21 — PRESUMPTION AS TO OFFENCES
In a prosecution for an offence under this chapter, if it is proved that the accused was not a member of SC/ST and the victim was, the court shall presume that the accused committed the offence.

RELIEF AND REHABILITATION:
The Rules prescribe monetary relief to victims:
1. For murder: Rs 8,25,000 (minimum)
2. For rape: Rs 5,00,000 (minimum)
3. For grievous hurt: Rs 1,00,000 to Rs 4,12,500
4. For arson: Cost of rebuilding + Rs 1,00,000
5. For social boycott: Rs 1,00,000

PREVENTION OF CORRUPTION ACT, 1988 (As amended in 2018)

SECTION 7 — PUBLIC SERVANT TAKING GRATIFICATION OTHER THAN LEGAL REMUNERATION
Any public servant who obtains, accepts, or attempts to obtain from any person, for himself or for any other person, any undue advantage as a motive or reward — imprisonment not less than 3 years, extendable to 7 years, and fine.

SECTION 7A — TAKING UNDUE ADVANTAGE TO INFLUENCE PUBLIC SERVANT (NEW)
Any person who gives or promises to give an undue advantage to another person with intent to induce a public servant to perform or not perform a public duty improperly — imprisonment not less than 3 years, extendable to 7 years, and fine.

SECTION 8 — PERSON WHO GIVES BRIBES (BRIBE GIVER)
Any person who gives or promises to give any undue advantage to a public servant — imprisonment up to 7 years, or fine, or both.
EXCEPTION: A person compelled to give a bribe shall not be guilty if he reports the matter within 7 days.

SECTION 9 — TAKING BRIBE BY A COMMERCIAL ORGANISATION
If any person associated with a commercial organisation gives or promises to give any undue advantage to a public servant — the commercial organisation shall be punishable with fine.

SECTION 13 — CRIMINAL MISCONDUCT BY PUBLIC SERVANT
A public servant commits criminal misconduct if he:
(a) dishonestly or fraudulently misappropriates or converts property entrusted to him;
(b) intentionally enriches himself illicitly during the period of his office.
Punishment: Imprisonment not less than 4 years, extendable to 10 years, and fine.

SECTION 17A — PREVIOUS SANCTION FOR INQUIRY/INVESTIGATION (NEW)
No police officer shall conduct any inquiry or investigation into any offence alleged to have been committed by a public servant who is or was serving in connection with the affairs of the Union or State, except with the previous approval of the authority competent to remove the said public servant from office.

SECTION 19 — PREVIOUS SANCTION NECESSARY FOR PROSECUTION
No court shall take cognizance of an offence punishable under Sections 7, 11, 13, and 15 alleged to have been committed by a public servant, except with the previous sanction of the authority competent to remove him.

DISPROPORTIONATE ASSETS CASE:
Under Section 13(1)(b), if a public servant is found in possession of pecuniary resources or property disproportionate to his known sources of income, and he cannot satisfactorily account for such resources, he is guilty of criminal misconduct.

ARMS ACT, 1959 — KEY PROVISIONS

SECTION 3 — LICENCE FOR ACQUISITION AND POSSESSION OF FIREARMS
No person shall acquire, have in his possession, or carry any firearm or ammunition unless he holds a licence.

SECTION 4 — LICENCE FOR MANUFACTURE, SALE, ETC.
No person shall sell, transfer, convert, repair, test, or prove any firearm or ammunition unless he holds a licence.

SECTION 25 — PUNISHMENT FOR CERTAIN OFFENCES
(1) Acquiring or possessing any firearms or ammunition without licence — imprisonment not less than 1 year, extendable to 3 years, and fine.
(1A) Acquiring or possessing prohibited arms or prohibited ammunition — imprisonment not less than 5 years, extendable to 10 years, and fine.
(1B) Possessing with intent to use against sovereignty or integrity of India — imprisonment not less than 7 years, extendable to imprisonment for life, and fine.

SECTION 27 — PUNISHMENT FOR USING ARMS
(1) Using any arm or ammunition in contravention of this Act — imprisonment not less than 3 years, extendable to 7 years, and fine.
(2) If someone is convicted under NDPS Act along with Arms Act — not less than 10 years, extendable to imprisonment for life, and fine.
(3) If used to commit scheduled offence — imprisonment not less than 7 years, extendable to imprisonment for life, and fine.

NARCOTIC DRUGS AND PSYCHOTROPIC SUBSTANCES ACT, 1985 (NDPS ACT)

SECTION 8 — PROHIBITION OF CERTAIN OPERATIONS
No person shall cultivate any coca plant, opium poppy or cannabis plant, or produce, manufacture, possess, sell, purchase, transport, store, or consume any narcotic drug or psychotropic substance except for medical or scientific purposes and in the manner and to the extent provided by this Act.

SECTION 20 — PUNISHMENT FOR CANNABIS
(a) Small quantity: Rigorous imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to Rs 10,000, or both.
(b) Quantity more than small but less than commercial: RI up to 10 years and fine up to Rs 1 lakh.
(c) Commercial quantity: RI not less than 10 years, extendable to 20 years, and fine not less than Rs 1 lakh, extendable to Rs 2 lakh.

SECTION 21 — PUNISHMENT FOR MANUFACTURED DRUGS
Similar graded punishment structure as above based on small/intermediate/commercial quantity.

SECTION 22 — PUNISHMENT FOR PSYCHOTROPIC SUBSTANCES
Similar graded punishment structure.

SECTION 27 — PUNISHMENT FOR CONSUMPTION
(a) Cocaine, morphine, heroin: Imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to Rs 20,000, or both.
(b) Other substances: Imprisonment up to 6 months, or fine up to Rs 10,000, or both.

SECTION 31A — DEATH PENALTY FOR REPEAT OFFENDERS
A person convicted for offences involving commercial quantity who is again convicted — may be punished with death.

SECTION 37 — OFFENCES TO BE COGNIZABLE AND NON-BAILABLE
All offences under this Act are cognizable and non-bailable. Bail is extremely restricted — the court must be satisfied that there are reasonable grounds for believing that the accused is not guilty and not likely to commit any offence while on bail.

SECTION 50 — SEARCH OF PERSON
Before searching any person, the officer must inform the person of his right to be searched before a Gazetted Officer or a Magistrate. Non-compliance with Section 50 makes the search illegal and evidence inadmissible.

SECTION 52A — DISPOSAL OF SEIZED DRUGS
The seized drugs shall be stored, preserved and disposed of after drawing samples in the presence of a Magistrate.
