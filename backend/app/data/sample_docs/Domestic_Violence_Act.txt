PROTECTION OF WOMEN FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT, 2005 - KEY SECTIONS

SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS

(a) "Aggrieved Person" means any woman who is, or has been, in an intimate relationship with the respondent and who alleges to have been subjected to any act of domestic violence by the respondent.

(b) "Domestic Violence" includes:
- Acts of physical violence
- Acts of sexual abuse
- Acts of verbal and emotional abuse
- Acts of economic abuse

SECTION 3 — RIGHT TO PROTECTION FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE
(1) Every woman who is subjected to domestic violence has the right to protection from domestic violence.
(2) The protection shall include:
(a) Protection orders;
(b) Residence orders;
(c) Monetary reliefs;
(d) Custody orders;
(e) Compensation orders.

SECTION 12 — PROTECTION ORDERS
(1) A Protection Order passed by the Court may direct the respondent:
(a) Not to commit acts of domestic violence;
(b) Not to cause injury by any act of omission or commission;
(c) To abstain from abusing or threatening the aggrieved person;
(d) To remove weapons if any from his possession.

SECTION 18 — RESIDENCE ORDERS
On receiving a complaint of domestic violence, the Court may:
(1) Pass an order restraining the respondent from dispossessing or in any manner dealing with the property;
(2) Pass an order to grant possession of the residence to the aggrieved person.

SECTION 20 — MONETARY RELIEF
(1) The Court may direct the respondent to pay:
(a) Compensation for loss of earnings;
(b) Medical expenses;
(c) Cost of proceedings;
(d) Any other cost incurred due to domestic violence.

HOW TO FILE A COMPLAINT:
1. Approach the Magistrate Court in your district
2. Or file with the Protection Officer appointed under this Act
3. You can file in person or through an advocate
4. No fee is charged for filing a complaint
5. Can be filed orally or in writing
