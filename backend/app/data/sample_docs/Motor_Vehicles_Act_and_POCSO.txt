MOTOR VEHICLES ACT, 1988 (as amended 2019) — KEY PROVISIONS

CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY

SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS
(28) "Motor vehicle" means any mechanically propelled vehicle adapted for use upon roads
(21) "Light motor vehicle" means a transport vehicle or omnibus the gross vehicle weight of which does not exceed 7500 kg
(47) "Transport vehicle" means a public service vehicle, a goods carriage, an educational institution bus or a private service vehicle

CHAPTER II — LICENSING OF DRIVERS

SECTION 3 — NECESSITY FOR DRIVING LICENCE
No person shall drive a motor vehicle in any public place unless he holds an effective driving licence.

SECTION 4 — AGE LIMIT IN CONNECTION WITH DRIVING
(1) No person under the age of eighteen years shall drive a motor vehicle in any public place.
(2) No person under the age of twenty years shall drive a transport vehicle in any public place.
(3) A person between 16-18 years can drive a motorcycle with engine capacity not exceeding 50cc.

SECTION 5 — RESPONSIBILITY OF OWNERS OF MOTOR VEHICLES FOR CONTRAVENTION OF SECTION 3
The owner of a motor vehicle shall not cause or permit any person who does not satisfy the requirements of Section 3 to drive the vehicle.

SECTION 7 — LEARNER'S LICENCE
(1) Any person who is not disqualified and has attained the prescribed age may apply for a learner's licence.
(2) A learner's licence shall be valid for 6 months from the date of issue.
(3) A learner must be accompanied by a person holding a valid driving licence at all times while driving.

CHAPTER III — REGISTRATION OF MOTOR VEHICLES

SECTION 39 — NECESSITY FOR REGISTRATION
No person shall drive any motor vehicle and no owner shall cause or permit any motor vehicle to be driven in any public place unless the vehicle is registered.

CHAPTER IV — CONTROL OF TRAFFIC

PENALTIES FOR TRAFFIC OFFENCES (As amended by Motor Vehicles Amendment Act, 2019):

General Offences (Section 177):
- Fine: Rs 500 (first offence), Rs 1,500 (subsequent)

Driving without Licence (Section 181):
- Fine: Rs 5,000 (increased from Rs 500)

Driving without Insurance (Section 196):
- Fine: Rs 2,000 (increased from Rs 1,000) and/or imprisonment up to 3 months

Over-speeding (Section 183):
- For LMV: Rs 1,000-2,000 (first offence), Rs 2,000-4,000 (subsequent)
- For medium/heavy vehicles: Rs 2,000-4,000

Drunk Driving (Section 185):
- First offence: Imprisonment up to 6 months and/or fine up to Rs 10,000
- Second offence (within 3 years): Imprisonment up to 2 years and/or fine up to Rs 15,000
- Blood alcohol limit: 30 mg per 100 ml of blood

Dangerous Driving (Section 184):
- First offence: Imprisonment 6 months to 1 year and/or fine Rs 1,000-5,000
- Second offence: Imprisonment up to 2 years and/or fine up to Rs 10,000

Using Mobile Phone While Driving (Section 184):
- Fine: Rs 1,000-5,000

Driving Without Seatbelt (Section 194B):
- Fine: Rs 1,000

Not Wearing Helmet (Section 194D):
- Fine: Rs 1,000 and disqualification for 3 months

Juvenile Driving (Section 199A — NEW):
- Owner/guardian: Fine Rs 25,000 and imprisonment up to 3 years
- Registration of the vehicle may be cancelled
- Juvenile to be tried under Juvenile Justice Act

Racing/Speed Testing (Section 189):
- Fine: Rs 5,000 (increased from Rs 500)

Overloading (Section 194):
- Rs 2,000 per extra tonne + Rs 1,000 per additional extra tonne

Not Giving Way to Emergency Vehicles (Section 194E):
- Fine: Rs 10,000

Driving Without Permit (Section 192A):
- Fine up to Rs 10,000 and/or imprisonment up to 6 months

CHAPTER V — INSURANCE

SECTION 146 — NECESSITY FOR INSURANCE AGAINST THIRD PARTY RISK
No person shall use or allow to be used a motor vehicle in a public place unless there is a policy of insurance in force.

SECTION 147 — REQUIREMENTS OF POLICIES OF INSURANCE
A policy of insurance must be issued by an authorized insurer and must insure against any liability in respect of death or bodily injury to any person caused by or arising out of the use of the vehicle in a public place.

SECTION 163A — SPECIAL PROVISIONS AS TO PAYMENT OF COMPENSATION ON STRUCTURED FORMULA BASIS
The owner or authorized insurer shall pay in case of death or permanent disablement arising out of an accident an amount based on a structured formula (no-fault liability — compensation without proving negligence):
- Death: Rs 50,000 (minimum, may be higher based on age and income)
- Permanent total disablement: Rs 25,000 (minimum)

SECTION 166 — APPLICATION FOR COMPENSATION
Application for compensation shall be made to the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT).

SECTION 168 — AWARD OF THE CLAIMS TRIBUNAL
The Claims Tribunal shall determine the compensation and issue an award specifying the amount and the person(s) liable to pay.

COMPUTATION OF COMPENSATION (Sarla Verma vs DTC, 2009):
The Supreme Court laid down a structured formula:
1. Annual Income of Deceased × Multiplier Factor (based on age) = Compensation
2. Deduction for personal expenses (1/3 if survived by wife and children, 1/2 if survived by only parents)
3. Multiplier ranges from 18 (for age 15-20) to 5 (for age 65+)
4. Additional: Rs 15,000 for loss of estate, Rs 15,000 for funeral expenses, Rs 1,00,000 for loss of consortium
5. Income includes future prospects (50% addition if below 40 years, 30% if 40-50 years)

NATIONAL HIGHWAYS AUTHORITY OF INDIA (Toll):
- Cars: Variable toll rates
- Exemptions: President, PM, CJI, Defence vehicles, ambulances, funeral vehicles
- FASTag is mandatory for all vehicles

HIT AND RUN CASES:
Under the Motor Vehicles Amendment Act 2019:
- Compensation for death: Rs 2,00,000 (increased from Rs 25,000)
- Compensation for grievous hurt: Rs 50,000 (increased from Rs 12,500)
- Central Government's Solatium Fund provides immediate relief

POCSO — PROTECTION OF CHILDREN FROM SEXUAL OFFENCES ACT, 2012

PURPOSE: To protect children (below 18 years) from sexual assault, sexual harassment and pornography offences.

SECTION 3 — PENETRATIVE SEXUAL ASSAULT
A person commits penetrative sexual assault if he penetrates his penis or inserts any object or body part into the vagina, mouth, urethra or anus of a child, or makes the child do so with him or any other person.
Punishment (Section 4): Not less than 10 years imprisonment, extendable to imprisonment for life, and fine.

SECTION 5 — AGGRAVATED PENETRATIVE SEXUAL ASSAULT
When penetrative sexual assault is committed by:
(a) a police officer within the limits of the police station;
(b) a member of the armed forces;
(c) a public servant;
(d) the management or staff of a jail, protection home, hospital, educational institution;
(e) a relative, guardian, teacher, or person in a position of trust;
(f) committed during communal or sectarian violence;
(g) committed on a child below 12 years;
(h) committed by a person previously convicted;
(i) when the child becomes pregnant;
(j) committed by a gang;
(k) using deadly weapons, fire, heated substance, or corrosive substance.
Punishment (Section 6): Not less than 20 years imprisonment, extendable to imprisonment for life (remaining natural life) or death, and fine.

SECTION 7 — SEXUAL ASSAULT
When a person touches the vagina, penis, anus, breast of a child or makes the child touch such body part, with sexual intent — not less than 3 years imprisonment, extendable to 5 years, and fine.

SECTION 9 — AGGRAVATED SEXUAL ASSAULT
Same aggravating factors as Section 5, but for non-penetrative sexual assault.
Punishment (Section 10): Not less than 5 years imprisonment, extendable to 7 years, and fine.

SECTION 11 — SEXUAL HARASSMENT
(i) Uttering any word or making any sound or gesture with sexual intent;
(ii) Making a child exhibit his body or any part of his body;
(iii) Showing any object to a child in any form for pornographic purposes;
(iv) Repeatedly following or watching or contacting a child through electronic means;
(v) Threatening to use the child's real or fabricated depictions through electronic media.
Punishment (Section 12): Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine.

SECTION 13 — USE OF CHILD FOR PORNOGRAPHIC PURPOSES
Imprisonment up to 5 years and fine. If the material is distributed — up to 7 years and fine.

SECTION 14 — USING CHILD FOR PORNOGRAPHIC PURPOSES
First conviction: Up to 5 years and fine. Subsequent: Up to 7 years and fine.
If results in penetrative/aggravated assault: Life imprisonment.

SECTION 19 — REPORTING OF OFFENCES
(1) Any person who has apprehension that an offence is likely to be committed or has knowledge that an offence has been committed shall provide information to:
(a) the Special Juvenile Police Unit; or
(b) the local police.
(2) Failure to report is punishable with imprisonment up to 6 months and/or fine.

SECTION 21 — MEDIA REPORTING
No person shall make any report or comment on a child that discloses the identity of the child. Violation: Imprisonment up to 1 year and/or fine.

SECTION 29 — PRESUMPTION AS TO CERTAIN OFFENCES
When a person is prosecuted for certain offences, the Special Court shall presume that such person has committed the offence unless the contrary is proved.

SECTION 30 — PRESUMPTION OF CULPABLE MENTAL STATE
When an offence requires a culpable mental state on the part of the accused, the Special Court shall presume the existence of such mental state. Burden of proof is on the accused to prove the contrary.

SECTION 33 — PROCEDURE FOR RECORDING STATEMENT OF CHILD
(1) Statement shall be recorded as spoken by the child;
(2) The Magistrate shall create a child-friendly environment;
(3) The child shall not be called to the court repeatedly;
(4) No aggressive questioning or character assassination;
(5) The identity of the child shall not be disclosed to the media.

SECTION 35 — SPECIAL COURT TO COMPLETE TRIAL WITHIN ONE YEAR
The Special Court shall complete the trial within a period of one year from the date of taking cognizance of the offence.
