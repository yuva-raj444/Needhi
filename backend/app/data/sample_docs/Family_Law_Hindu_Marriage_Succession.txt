HINDU MARRIAGE ACT, 1955 — KEY PROVISIONS

SECTION 2 — APPLICATION
This Act applies to any person who is a Hindu by religion, including Virashaiva, Lingayat, followers of Brahmo, Prarthana or Arya Samaj, any Buddhist, Jain or Sikh, and any person who is not a Muslim, Christian, Parsi or Jew.

SECTION 5 — CONDITIONS FOR A HINDU MARRIAGE
A marriage may be solemnized between any two Hindus, if the following conditions are fulfilled:
(i) Neither party has a spouse living at the time of the marriage (monogamy);
(ii) At the time of the marriage, neither party is incapable of giving a valid consent due to unsoundness of mind, or suffers from mental disorder of such a kind or to such an extent as to be unfit for marriage and the procreation of children, or has been subject to recurrent attacks of insanity;
(iii) The bridegroom has completed the age of twenty-one years and the bride the age of eighteen years at the time of the marriage;
(iv) The parties are not within the degrees of prohibited relationship unless the custom governing each of them permits marriage between the two;
(v) The parties are not sapindas of each other, unless the custom governing each of them permits marriage between the two.

SECTION 7 — CEREMONIES FOR A HINDU MARRIAGE
(1) A Hindu marriage may be solemnized in accordance with the customary rites and ceremonies of either party.
(2) Where the ceremonies include Saptapadi (seven steps before the sacred fire), the marriage becomes complete and binding when the seventh step is taken.

SECTION 9 — RESTITUTION OF CONJUGAL RIGHTS
When either the husband or the wife has, without reasonable excuse, withdrawn from the society of the other, the aggrieved party may apply to the district court for restitution of conjugal rights.

SECTION 10 — JUDICIAL SEPARATION
Either party to a marriage may present a petition for judicial separation on the same grounds as for divorce under Section 13.

SECTION 11 — VOID MARRIAGES
Any marriage solemnized after the commencement of this Act shall be null and void if it contravenes conditions (i), (iv), or (v) of Section 5.

SECTION 12 — VOIDABLE MARRIAGES
A marriage shall be voidable and may be annulled by a decree of nullity if:
(a) the marriage has not been consummated owing to the impotence of the respondent;
(b) the marriage is in contravention of Section 5(ii);
(c) the consent was obtained by force or fraud;
(d) the respondent was at the time of the marriage pregnant by some person other than the petitioner.

SECTION 13 — GROUNDS FOR DIVORCE
(1) Any marriage may be dissolved by a decree of divorce on the petition of either party on the ground that the other party:
(i) has had voluntary sexual intercourse with any person other than his or her spouse (adultery);
(ia) has treated the petitioner with cruelty;
(ib) has deserted the petitioner for a continuous period of not less than two years;
(ii) has ceased to be a Hindu by conversion to another religion;
(iii) has been incurably of unsound mind;
(iv) has been suffering from a virulent form of leprosy;
(v) has been suffering from venereal disease in a communicable form;
(vi) has renounced the world by entering any religious order;
(vii) has not been heard of as being alive for a period of seven years.

(2) Additional grounds available to wife only:
(i) the husband has been guilty of rape, sodomy or bestiality;
(ii) the wife was married before age 15 and repudiated the marriage before turning 18;
(iii) the husband has married again (in violation of the Act) or has a living wife.

SECTION 13A — ALTERNATE RELIEF IN DIVORCE PROCEEDINGS
In any proceeding for dissolution of marriage, the court may make a decree for judicial separation instead if it considers just and proper.

SECTION 13B — DIVORCE BY MUTUAL CONSENT
(1) Both parties may jointly present a petition for dissolution of marriage on the grounds that they have been living separately for a period of one year or more and have mutually agreed that the marriage should be dissolved.
(2) After six months from the date of presentation of the petition, the motion for divorce may be made. The court shall, on being satisfied, pass a decree of divorce.
(3) The Supreme Court has ruled (Amardeep Singh vs Harveen Kaur, 2017) that the 6-month cooling-off period can be waived by courts in exceptional circumstances.

SECTION 14 — NO DIVORCE WITHIN ONE YEAR
No petition for divorce shall be presented within one year of the date of marriage. Exception: Cases of exceptional hardship or depravity.

SECTION 15 — WHEN DIVORCED PERSONS MAY MARRY AGAIN
When a marriage has been dissolved by a decree of divorce and the time for appeal has expired or the appeal has been dismissed, it shall be lawful for either party to marry again.

SECTION 24 — MAINTENANCE PENDENTE LITE AND EXPENSES OF PROCEEDINGS
Where in any proceeding it appears to the court that either the wife or the husband has no independent income sufficient for support, the court may order the respondent to pay maintenance and expenses of the proceedings as the court may deem just.

SECTION 25 — PERMANENT ALIMONY AND MAINTENANCE
(1) Any court exercising jurisdiction may, at the time of passing any decree or at any time subsequent thereto, order that the respondent shall pay to the applicant for maintenance and support such gross sum or monthly sum as the court may deem just.
(2) The court shall have due regard to the respondent's own income and other property, if any.

SECTION 26 — CUSTODY OF CHILDREN
In any proceeding, the court may make such interim orders as it may deem just and proper with respect to the custody, maintenance and education of minor children. The welfare of the child shall be the paramount consideration.

SPECIAL MARRIAGE ACT, 1954 — KEY PROVISIONS

PURPOSE: Provides for civil marriage between parties belonging to different religions or who do not wish a religious ceremony.

SECTION 4 — CONDITIONS RELATING TO SOLEMNIZATION
A marriage may be solemnized under this Act if:
(a) Neither party has a living spouse;
(b) Neither party is of unsound mind;
(c) The male has completed 21 years and the female 18 years;
(d) The parties are not within the degrees of prohibited relationship (unless custom permits);
(e) Both parties give their free consent.

SECTION 5 — NOTICE OF INTENDED MARRIAGE
Parties must give 30 days' notice to the Marriage Officer in the district where at least one party has resided for at least 30 days. The notice is displayed for 30 days for objections.

SECTION 15 — REGISTRATION OF MARRIAGES CELEBRATED IN OTHER FORMS
Any marriage celebrated under any other form (religious) may be registered under this Act if both parties agree.

SECTION 27 — DIVORCE
The grounds for divorce are similar to the Hindu Marriage Act:
(a) adultery, (b) desertion for 2+ years, (c) imprisonment for 7+ years, (d) cruelty, (e) unsound mind, (f) venereal disease, (g) leprosy, (h) not heard alive for 7+ years, (i) non-resumption of cohabitation after judicial separation for 1+ year.

SECTION 28 — DIVORCE BY MUTUAL CONSENT
Both parties who have lived separately for 1+ year and mutually agree may petition for divorce. The 6-month cooling-off period applies.

ADVANTAGES OF SPECIAL MARRIAGE ACT:
1. No religious conversion required for inter-faith marriages
2. Provides legal recognition to the marriage
3. Marriage is registered under a civil law
4. Same divorce provisions available
5. No dowry exchange is mandatory

HINDU SUCCESSION ACT, 1956 — KEY PROVISIONS (as amended in 2005)

SECTION 6 — DEVOLUTION OF INTEREST IN COPARCENARY PROPERTY (AMENDED 2005)
(1) The daughter of a coparcener shall by birth become a coparcener in her own right in the same manner as the son.
(2) The daughter shall have the same rights in the coparcenary property as she would have had if she had been a son.
(3) The daughter shall have the same rights and liabilities as that of a son.
(This 2005 amendment is a landmark change giving daughters equal rights in ancestral property)

SECTION 8 — GENERAL RULES OF SUCCESSION FOR HINDU MALES
The property of a Hindu male dying intestate shall devolve according to the following:
Class I heirs: Son, Daughter, Widow, Mother, Son of a predeceased son, Daughter of a predeceased son, Son of a predeceased daughter, Daughter of a predeceased daughter, Widow of a predeceased son, Son of a predeceased son of a predeceased son, Daughter of a predeceased son of a predeceased son, Widow of a predeceased son of a predeceased son.
Class I heirs get preference over Class II heirs.
If no Class I or II heirs: Agnates → Cognates.

SECTION 14 — PROPERTY OF A FEMALE HINDU TO BE HER ABSOLUTE PROPERTY
Any property possessed by a female Hindu, whether acquired before or after the commencement of this Act, shall be held by her as full owner thereof and not as a limited owner.

SECTION 15 — GENERAL RULES OF SUCCESSION FOR HINDU FEMALES
The property of a Hindu female dying intestate shall devolve:
(a) upon the sons and daughters (including children of any predeceased son or daughter) and the husband;
(b) upon the heirs of the husband;
(c) upon the mother and father;
(d) upon the heirs of the father;
(e) upon the heirs of the mother.

SECTION 30 — TESTAMENTARY SUCCESSION
Any Hindu may dispose of by will any property which is capable of being so disposed of in accordance with the Indian Succession Act, 1925.

IMPORTANT: Self-acquired property can be willed to anyone. Ancestral/coparcenary property — only the share of the deceased can be willed.

MAINTENANCE AND WELFARE OF PARENTS AND SENIOR CITIZENS ACT, 2007

SECTION 4 — OBLIGATION TO MAINTAIN PARENTS AND SENIOR CITIZENS
(1) A senior citizen including parent who is unable to maintain himself from his own earning or property owned by him, shall be entitled to make an application for maintenance.
(2) Children and relatives are obligated to maintain senior citizens.

SECTION 5 — APPLICATION FOR MAINTENANCE
An application for maintenance may be made by:
(a) A senior citizen or parent himself;
(b) Any organisation or person authorized by the senior citizen;
(c) The Tribunal may take cognizance suo motu.

SECTION 9 — MAINTENANCE TRIBUNAL
The State Government shall establish one or more Maintenance Tribunals in every Sub-Division.

SECTION 23 — TRANSFER OF PROPERTY BY SENIOR CITIZEN TO BE VOID IN CERTAIN CIRCUMSTANCES
If a senior citizen has transferred his property by way of gift or otherwise, subject to the condition that the transferee shall provide basic amenities and physical needs, and such transferee refuses or fails to provide such amenities, the said transfer shall be deemed to have been made by fraud and the Tribunal may declare the transfer as void.

MAXIMUM MAINTENANCE: The Tribunal may order maintenance up to Rs 10,000 per month (varies by state, some states have increased this).
