RIGHT OF CHILDREN TO FREE AND COMPULSORY EDUCATION ACT, 2009 (RTE ACT)

SECTION 3 — RIGHT OF CHILD TO FREE AND COMPULSORY EDUCATION
(1) Every child of the age of six to fourteen years shall have a right to free and compulsory education in a neighbourhood school till completion of elementary education.
(2) No child shall be liable to pay any kind of fee or charges or expenses.

SECTION 4 — SPECIAL PROVISIONS FOR CHILDREN NOT ADMITTED TO OR WHO HAVE NOT COMPLETED ELEMENTARY EDUCATION
A child above six years who has not been admitted to any school shall be admitted in a class appropriate to his or her age.

SECTION 12 — EXTENT OF SCHOOL'S RESPONSIBILITY FOR FREE AND COMPULSORY EDUCATION
(1)(c) Every unaided private school and specified category school shall admit in class I, to the extent of at least 25% of the strength of that class, children belonging to weaker section and disadvantaged group in the neighbourhood. The school shall provide free and compulsory education till its completion.

SECTION 13 — NO CAPITATION FEE AND SCREENING PROCEDURE FOR ADMISSION
(1) No school shall collect any capitation fee while admitting a child.
(2) No screening procedure shall be conducted for admission.

SECTION 16 — NO HOLDING BACK AND EXPULSION
No child admitted shall be held back in any class or expelled from school till the completion of elementary education.

SECTION 17 — NO CORPORAL PUNISHMENT
No child shall be subjected to physical punishment or mental harassment. Violation: Disciplinary action under service rules.

SECTION 23 — QUALIFICATIONS FOR TEACHERS
Teachers must possess minimum qualifications as laid down by academic authority (NCTE). Teachers who do not possess minimum qualifications shall acquire them within 5 years.

SECTION 29 — CURRICULUM AND EVALUATION
The curriculum shall be laid down by the academic authority and shall include: all-round development of the child, building the child's knowledge, potentiality and talent, development of physical and mental abilities, learning through activities, discovery and exploration, and comprehensive and continuous evaluation.

LABOUR CODES 2020 — KEY PROVISIONS
(Four Labour Codes consolidating 29 Central Labour Laws)

CODE ON WAGES, 2019

SECTION 3 — MINIMUM WAGES
The Central Government shall fix the floor wage, and no State Government shall fix the minimum wage below the floor wage.

SECTION 5 — PAYMENT OF WAGES
(1) Wages shall be paid in current coin or currency notes or by cheque or by crediting to the bank account.
(2) The wage period shall be either daily, weekly, fortnightly or monthly.
(3) Where the wage period is monthly, wages shall be paid before the expiry of the 7th day of the succeeding month. If less than 1000 workers, before the 10th day.

SECTION 6 — DEDUCTIONS FROM WAGES
Deductions may be made only on account of:
(a) fines imposed
(b) absence from duty
(c) damage to or loss of goods
(d) housing accommodation
(e) amenities and services
(f) recovery of advances
(g) income tax
(h) court orders
(i) provident fund contributions
(j) insurance premium

SECTION 17 — BONUS
Every employer shall pay a bonus of not less than 8.33% (minimum) of the wages or Rs 100 (whichever is higher) to every employee who has worked for not less than 30 days in a year. Maximum bonus: 20% of wages.

INDUSTRIAL RELATIONS CODE, 2020

SECTION 2(k) — WORKER
"Worker" means any person employed in any industry, excluding those employed in managerial or administrative capacity or supervisory capacity drawing wages exceeding Rs 18,000 per month.

SECTION 14 — PROHIBITION OF STRIKES AND LOCK-OUTS
(1) No worker shall go on strike without giving a 60-day notice (in public utility services) or 14-day notice (in other establishments).
(2) No employer shall declare a lock-out without similar notice.
(3) No strike or lock-out during pendency of conciliation proceedings and 7 days after conclusion.

SECTION 77 — RETRENCHMENT
(1) No workman employed in any industrial establishment who has been in continuous service for not less than one year shall be retrenched unless:
(a) The workman has been given one month's notice in writing or wages in lieu thereof;
(b) The workman has been paid retrenchment compensation equal to 15 days' average pay for every completed year of continuous service;
(c) Notice has been served on the appropriate Government.

SECTION 78 — PRIOR PERMISSION FOR RETRENCHMENT IN CERTAIN ESTABLISHMENTS
Every employer of an establishment employing 300 or more workers shall obtain prior permission of the appropriate Government for retrenchment. (Threshold increased from 100 to 300)

SECTION 84 — UNFAIR LABOUR PRACTICE
Both employers and workers/trade unions can commit unfair labour practices:
By employers: Interfering with workers' rights, discriminating against union members, closing establishment to break a strike.
By workers: Intimidation, go-slow, coercion of non-members.

OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY, HEALTH AND WORKING CONDITIONS CODE, 2020

SECTION 6 — DUTIES OF EMPLOYER
(1) Ensure a workplace free from hazards;
(2) Provide free annual health check-ups for prescribed categories of workers;
(3) Issue appointment letters to every employee;
(4) Comply with provisions relating to dangerous operations.

SECTION 23 — WORKING HOURS
No worker shall be required or allowed to work in any establishment for more than 8 hours in a day. Maximum weekly hours: 48 hours.

SECTION 25 — WEEKLY HOLIDAYS
Every worker shall be given a holiday of one whole day in a week.

SECTION 26 — OVERTIME
For work beyond normal hours, the worker shall be entitled to wages at the rate of twice his ordinary rate of wages.

SECTION 32 — INTER-STATE MIGRANT WORKERS
(1) Every establishment employing inter-state migrant workers shall ensure: lump-sum allowance for journey, suitable working conditions, equal treatment as local workers.
(2) NEW: Annual return filing by employer regarding number and details of interstate migrant workers.

SOCIAL SECURITY CODE, 2020

SECTION 2(26) — EMPLOYEE
Includes any person employed on wages in any establishment including contract labour, gig workers and platform workers.

KEY SOCIAL SECURITY PROVISIONS:
1. EMPLOYEES' PROVIDENT FUND (EPF):
   - Applies to establishments employing 20+ workers
   - Employee contribution: 12% of basic wages
   - Employer contribution: 12% of basic wages (3.67% to EPF, 8.33% to EPS)
   - Withdrawal: On retirement (58 years), or for housing, medical, education, marriage

2. EMPLOYEES' STATE INSURANCE (ESI):
   - Applies to establishments employing 10+ workers
   - Applicable for employees earning up to Rs 21,000/month
   - Employee contribution: 0.75% of wages
   - Employer contribution: 3.25% of wages
   - Benefits: Medical, sickness, maternity, disablement, dependants' benefit

3. GRATUITY:
   - Payable to employee who has completed 5 years of continuous service
   - Amount: 15 days' wages for every completed year of service
   - Maximum: Rs 20,00,000
   - Payable on superannuation, retirement, resignation, death or disablement

4. MATERNITY BENEFIT:
   - 26 weeks paid leave for first two children
   - 12 weeks for third child onwards
   - 12 weeks for adopting mother/commissioning mother
   - Cannot be dismissed during maternity leave

5. GIG WORKERS AND PLATFORM WORKERS (NEW):
   - Defined for the first time in Indian law
   - Central Government may frame schemes for gig and platform workers
   - Social security fund to be created
   - Aggregators to contribute 1-2% of annual turnover

EMPLOYEES' COMPENSATION ACT, 1923 (formerly Workmen's Compensation Act)

SECTION 3 — EMPLOYER'S LIABILITY FOR COMPENSATION
If personal injury is caused to an employee by accident arising out of and in the course of his employment, the employer shall be liable to pay compensation.

COMPENSATION AMOUNTS:
(a) Death: An amount equal to 50% of monthly wages × relevant factor (based on age), or Rs 1,20,000, whichever is more.
(b) Permanent total disablement: An amount equal to 60% of monthly wages × relevant factor, or Rs 1,40,000, whichever is more.
(c) Permanent partial disablement: Percentage of compensation for total disablement proportionate to loss of earning capacity.
(d) Temporary disablement: 25% of monthly wages, payable half-monthly.

SECTION 4A — TIME LIMIT FOR PAYMENT
Compensation shall be paid within one month from the date it falls due. If not paid, simple interest @ 12% per annum shall be added. If there is no justification for delay, penalty up to 50% of the compensation amount.
