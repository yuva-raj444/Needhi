CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT, 2019 - KEY SECTIONS

SECTION 2(d) — CONSUMER
A consumer means any natural person who enters into a transaction, purchases or uses goods or services for personal use and not for any commercial purpose.

SECTION 2(k) — DEFECT
Defect means any fault, imperfection, shortcoming or inadequacy in the quality, nature and or manner of performance which is required to be maintained by or under any law for the time being in force or as claimed by the trader in any manner whatsoever in relation to goods or services.

SECTION 2(l) — UNFAIR TRADE PRACTICE
Unfair trade practice means a trade practice which, for the purpose of promoting the sale, use or supply of any goods or for the provision of any service, adopts any unfair method or unfair or deceptive practice.

SECTION 14 — FUNCTIONS OF CENTRAL CONSUMER PROTECTION AUTHORITY
(1) The Central Authority shall perform the following functions, namely:
(a) Combating and preventing of misleading advertisements;
(b) Testing of goods and services;
(c) Investigation of complaints on violations of consumer rights.

SECTION 60 — JURISDICTION OF DISTRICT COMMISSION
(1) Subject to the other provisions of this Act, a District Commission shall have jurisdiction to entertain a complaint of a consumer if the value of goods or services and the compensation claimed exceeds one lakh rupees but does not exceed one crore rupees.

SECTION 61 — JURISDICTION OF STATE COMMISSION
(1) Subject to other provisions of this Act, a State Commission shall have jurisdiction to entertain a complaint if the value of goods or services and the compensation claimed exceeds one crore rupees but does not exceed ten crore rupees.

SECTION 2(j) — DEFICIENCY
Deficiency means any shortcoming, inadequacy or lack of fitness in the quality, nature and manner of performance which is required to be maintained by or under any law for the time being in force, or as claimed by the trader in relation to any goods or services.

REMEDIES FOR DEFECTIVE GOODS OR SERVICES
1. Replacement of goods
2. Repair of goods
3. Reduction in price
4. Compensation for loss
5. Removal of defect and its causes
6. Compensation for any harm caused
7. Refund of the price and cost of replacement
