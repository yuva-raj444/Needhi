ELECTION LAWS, VOTING RIGHTS AND POLITICAL PARTICIPATION IN INDIA
==================================================================

PART 1: REPRESENTATION OF THE PEOPLE ACT, 1950 AND 1951
=========================================================

VOTING RIGHTS:
Every Indian citizen above 18 years is eligible to vote.
No discrimination based on caste, religion, gender, class, or education.

ELECTION COMMISSION OF INDIA (ECI):
Constitutional body under Article 324.
Superintendence, direction, and control of elections.
Website: eci.gov.in | Helpline: 1950

CHIEF ELECTION COMMISSIONER:
Independent — can only be removed like a Supreme Court judge.
(PENDING: Supreme Court directed more transparent appointment process)

PART 2: VOTER REGISTRATION
============================

WHO CAN REGISTER:
- Indian citizen
- 18 years or above
- Ordinary resident of the constituency

HOW TO REGISTER:
1. Online: voters.eci.gov.in (Form 6)
2. Offline: Form 6 to Electoral Registration Officer (ERO) of your constituency
3. Through Common Service Centre (CSC)

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Proof of identity (Aadhaar, passport, etc.)
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement, etc.)
- Date of birth proof (if disputed)

ELECTORAL ROLL:
Published by Election Commission — you can check your name at:
electoralsearch.eci.gov.in

PHOTO VOTER ID CARD (EPIC):
Not mandatory for voting — any of 12 alternative documents can be used.
Alternative documents: Aadhaar, passport, PAN, service identity card, etc.

PART 3: ELECTION PROCESS
==========================

GENERAL ELECTIONS (LOK SABHA):
Every 5 years. 543 constituencies. First Past the Post system.

STATE ELECTIONS (VIDHAN SABHA):
Every 5 years per state.

RAJYA SABHA:
Members elected by State Legislative Assembly MLAs.
Not directly elected by people.

ELECTION DATES ANNOUNCED BY ECI:
Model Code of Conduct (MCC) comes into effect from the date of announcement.

MODEL CODE OF CONDUCT (MCC):
Rules for political parties and candidates during election period:
- No new government schemes or freebies announced
- Government resources cannot be used for campaign
- Religious/caste-based appeals prohibited
- Polling booth locations cannot be changed without permission

PART 4: CANDIDATE ELIGIBILITY AND DISQUALIFICATION
====================================================

WHO CAN CONTEST ELECTIONS:
- Indian citizen
- 25 years for Lok Sabha / State Assembly
- 30 years for Rajya Sabha / Legislative Council
- Registered as voter in India

DISQUALIFICATION OF CANDIDATES (SECTION 8 REPRESENTATION OF PEOPLE ACT):
A person is disqualified if convicted and sentenced to imprisonment of 2 years
or more. Disqualification applies from date of conviction.
Duration: Period of imprisonment + 6 years after release.

ANTI-DEFECTION LAW (TENTH SCHEDULE, CONSTITUTION):
If an elected member voluntarily gives up membership of their party,
OR votes against the party's direction on a confidence/no-confidence motion,
they can be disqualified from their seat.
Decision by: Speaker of Lok Sabha / Speaker of Vidhan Sabha.

CRIMINAL BACKGROUND DISCLOSURE:
All candidates MUST declare criminal cases pending against them on their
nomination form. Voters have the right to know this information.
Available at myneta.info (voter information website).

PART 5: ELECTION OFFENCES AND CORRUPT PRACTICES
=================================================

CORRUPT PRACTICES (SECTION 123 RPA 1951):
These can invalidate the election result:
- Bribery (paying voters to vote)
- Undue influence (threatening voters)
- Making appeals on the basis of religion, race, caste, community, or language
- Publishing false statements about a candidate
- Booth capturing
- Hiring vehicles to bring voters to polling booth

PENALTIES:
Corrupt practices: Election can be set aside by High Court.
Candidate guilty of corrupt practice: Disqualified for 6 years.

ELECTION BRIBERY (DISTRIBUTING MONEY/GIFTS):
- Briber: Imprisonment up to 1 year or fine or both
- Voter accepting bribe: Same penalty
- Report bribery to local police or ECI's flying squad: cVIGIL App (official ECI app)

cVIGIL APP (ELECTION COMMISSION):
Citizens can report electoral violations through this app.
Available on Android and iOS.
Reports go directly to ECI's flying squad.

PART 6: RIGHT TO VOTE — PRACTICAL ASPECTS
==========================================

ELECTION HOLIDAYS:
Your employer MUST give you a paid holiday on the day of election
(Representation of the People Act). If employer refuses: Report to ECI.

VOTING PROCESS:
1. Check your name on electoral roll at electoralsearch.eci.gov.in
2. Find your polling booth
3. Go to booth with voter ID or any valid ID
4. Voter ID card is verified by polling officer
5. Fingers marked with indelible ink
6. Ballot paper / EVM (Electronic Voting Machine) button pressed

ELECTRONIC VOTING MACHINES (EVM):
Used in all Indian elections since 2000.
Supreme Court has upheld EVMs as reliable.
Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail (VPAPT) introduced for verification.

RIGHT TO REJECT (NOTA):
Voters can choose "None Of The Above" (NOTA) option if they don't want
to vote for any candidate. NOTA was introduced in 2013 as per Supreme Court order.
Note: Even if NOTA gets the highest votes, the candidate with highest valid votes wins.

PART 7: ELECTION DISPUTES AND REMEDIES
========================================

ELECTION PETITION:
If you believe the election was won through corrupt practices, you can
file an Election Petition in the High Court within 45 days of the result.

GROUNDS:
- Corrupt practice by winning candidate or their agent
- Non-compliance with election rules materially affecting result
- Candidate not qualified

RELIEF:
- Declaring the election void
- Declaring the petitioner as the elected candidate

CASE EXAMPLE: Famous election petitions include those challenging close
elections — High Court has powers to order fresh counting or fresh polls.

PART 8: RIGHT TO INFORMATION (RTI) IN ELECTIONS
=================================================

Voters have the right to know:
- Election expenditure statements of candidates
- Affidavits (assets, liabilities, criminal cases) of candidates
- Audit reports of political parties

All available at: affidavit.eci.gov.in and myneta.info

ELECTORAL BONDS (Current Status):
Supreme Court struck down the Electoral Bond scheme in February 2024
(Association for Democratic Reforms vs Union of India).
Bonds declared unconstitutional for violating right to information
and free and fair elections.

FUNDING OF ELECTIONS:
Maximum election expenditure limits set by ECI.
Candidates must maintain accounts and file expenditure statements.
Exceeding limit: Disqualification.
