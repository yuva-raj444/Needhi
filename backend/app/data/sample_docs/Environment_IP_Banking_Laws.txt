ENVIRONMENT AND POLLUTION LAWS IN INDIA

ENVIRONMENT (PROTECTION) ACT, 1986

SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS
(a) "Environment" includes water, air and land and the inter-relationship which exists among and between water, air and land, and human beings, other living creatures, plants, micro-organisms and property.

SECTION 3 — POWER OF CENTRAL GOVERNMENT TO TAKE MEASURES
The Central Government shall have the power to take all such measures as it deems necessary for the purpose of protecting and improving the quality of the environment.

SECTION 7 — NO PERSON SHALL CARRY ON INDUSTRY OR OPERATION WITHOUT PERMISSION
No person carrying on any industry, operation or process shall discharge or emit any environmental pollutant in excess of prescribed standards.

SECTION 15 — PENALTY FOR CONTRAVENTION
Whoever contravenes any provision of this Act — imprisonment up to 5 years, or fine up to Rs 1 lakh, or both. If the failure continues — additional fine up to Rs 5,000 per day. If the failure continues beyond 1 year — imprisonment up to 7 years.

WATER (PREVENTION AND CONTROL OF POLLUTION) ACT, 1974:
- Prohibits discharge of pollutants into water bodies without consent of State Pollution Control Board
- Penalty: Imprisonment 1.5 to 6 years and fine

AIR (PREVENTION AND CONTROL OF POLLUTION) ACT, 1981:
- Prohibits emission of air pollutants in excess of prescribed standards
- Industrial units in pollution control areas require consent from SPCB
- Penalty: Imprisonment up to 6 years, or fine up to Rs 5,000, or both

NOISE POLLUTION (REGULATION AND CONTROL) RULES, 2000:
- Ambient noise standards:
  Industrial area: 75 dB(A) day, 70 dB(A) night
  Commercial area: 65 dB(A) day, 55 dB(A) night
  Residential area: 55 dB(A) day, 45 dB(A) night
  Silence zone (near hospitals, schools): 50 dB(A) day, 40 dB(A) night
- Use of loudspeakers/public address systems prohibited between 10 PM and 6 AM
- Filing noise complaint: Approach local police or Pollution Control Board

SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT RULES, 2016:
- Segregation of waste at source into biodegradable, non-biodegradable and domestic hazardous waste is mandatory
- Bulk generators (restaurants, hotels, malls) shall segregate and process biodegradable waste on-site or hand over to authorized waste processors
- No burning of waste in open
- Littering in public places: Fine (varies by state)

E-WASTE (MANAGEMENT) RULES, 2016:
- Producers of electronic products must provide for collection and channelization of e-waste
- Consumers must not dispose of e-waste in dustbins or landfills
- Authorized dismantlers and recyclers only shall process e-waste

ENVIRONMENTAL CLEARANCE:
- Projects above prescribed thresholds require Environmental Clearance from MoEFCC (Central) or SEIAA (State)
- Process: Screening → Scoping → Public Hearing → Appraisal → Decision
- EIA Notification 2006 (as amended) prescribes categories of projects

NATIONAL GREEN TRIBUNAL (NGT):
- Established under NGT Act 2010
- Handles all civil cases related to environmental protection
- Can award compensation for damages to persons, property and environment
- Orders are binding; appeal to Supreme Court
- Limitation: 6 months from cause of action

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY LAWS IN INDIA

PATENTS ACT, 1970 (As amended 2005):
SECTION 2(1)(j): "Invention" means a new product or process involving an inventive step and capable of industrial application.
SECTION 3: What are not inventions — includes abstract theories, methods of agriculture/horticulture, any process for treatment of humans/animals, mere discovery of scientific principles, computer programs per se.
Patent term: 20 years from date of filing.
Patent can be revoked if not worked in India within 3 years of grant.

COPYRIGHT ACT, 1957:
SECTION 13: Copyright subsists in original literary, dramatic, musical and artistic works, cinematograph films, and sound recordings.
SECTION 14: Rights — reproduction, public performance, making copies, making translations/adaptations, communication to public.
SECTION 51: Infringement — when any person without licence does anything that the exclusive right of the copyright holder.
SECTION 63: Penalty — imprisonment not less than 6 months, extendable to 3 years, and fine not less than Rs 50,000, extendable to Rs 2 lakh.
Term: Life of author + 60 years.

TRADE MARKS ACT, 1999:
SECTION 2: "Trade mark" means a mark capable of being represented graphically and which is capable of distinguishing goods/services of one person from another.
Registration: 10 years, renewable for further periods of 10 years each.
SECTION 29: Infringement — using an identical or deceptively similar mark for identical or similar goods/services.
SECTION 103: Penalty for falsifying trademarks — imprisonment not less than 6 months, extendable to 3 years, and fine not less than Rs 50,000, extendable to Rs 2 lakh.
SECTION 105: Penalty for falsely applying trademark — same as Section 103.

BANKING AND FINANCIAL LAWS

SARFAESI ACT, 2002 (Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act):
SECTION 13: If a borrower makes any default in repayment and the account is classified as NPA:
(1) Bank must issue a 60-day notice to the borrower to discharge the liabilities.
(2) After 60 days, the bank may: take possession of the secured assets, sell the assets, appoint manager to manage the secured assets.
(3) This can be done WITHOUT intervention of court.

SECTION 17: Any person aggrieved by any of the measures taken by the bank under Section 13(4) may make an application to the Debts Recovery Tribunal (DRT) within 45 days.

INSOLVENCY AND BANKRUPTCY CODE, 2016 (IBC):
- Unified framework for insolvency resolution of corporates, partnership firms and individuals
- Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP): Must be completed within 180 days (extendable by 90 days, total maximum 330 days)
- NCLT (National Company Law Tribunal) is the adjudicating authority for corporate persons
- DRT for individuals and partnership firms
- Priority of claims: Insolvency resolution process costs → Secured creditors → Workers' dues (24 months) → Unsecured creditors → Government dues → Remaining debts

RECOVERY OF DEBTS AND BANKRUPTCY ACT, 1993:
- DRT handles recovery of debts due to banks and financial institutions
- For debts of Rs 20 lakh and above
- DRT must dispose of an application within 6 months

CONSUMER PROTECTION IN BANKING:
- Banking Ombudsman (appointed by RBI) handles complaints against banks
- Complaint can be filed online at https://cms.rbi.org.in
- Covers: Non-payment/delay in payment of cheques, non-adherence to fair practices code, levy of charges without notice, non-observance of RBI guidelines, etc.
- If unsatisfied with banking ombudsman's decision: Appeal to Deputy Governor, RBI within 30 days
