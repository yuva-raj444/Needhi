CYBER CRIMES AND INTERNET LAWS IN INDIA — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
===============================================================

PART 1: INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY ACT, 2000 (IT ACT) — DETAILED
==============================================================

The IT Act 2000 (amended in 2008) is the primary law governing cyber crimes in India.

KEY OFFENCES AND PUNISHMENTS:

SECTION 43 — DAMAGE TO COMPUTER (Civil Liability):
If anyone without permission:
- Accesses a computer, computer system or network
- Downloads, copies or extracts data
- Introduces viruses or contaminants
- Damages, deletes or alters data
- Denies access to authorised users (Denial of Service)
- Provides assistance for any of the above
PENALTY: Compensation up to Rs. 1 crore to the affected person.

SECTION 65 — TAMPERING WITH COMPUTER SOURCE CODE:
Concealing, destroying, altering computer source code.
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine up to Rs. 2 lakh OR BOTH.

SECTION 66 — COMPUTER RELATED OFFENCES (Hacking):
Dishonestly or fraudulently doing any act under Section 43.
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine up to Rs. 5 lakh OR BOTH.

SECTION 66A — OFFENSIVE MESSAGES (Struck down by Supreme Court in 2015):
This section was declared unconstitutional in Shreya Singhal vs Union of India (2015)
for violating Freedom of Speech. Sending offensive messages online is no longer
covered by this section. However, other sections still apply.

SECTION 66B — RECEIVING STOLEN COMPUTER RESOURCE:
Dishonestly receiving or retaining any stolen computer resource.
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh OR BOTH.

SECTION 66C — IDENTITY THEFT:
Fraudulently using another person's electronic signature, password, or
any other unique identification feature.
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh.

SECTION 66D — CHEATING BY PERSONATION (Online):
Cheating someone by personating another person through computer resources.
This covers fake profiles, pretending to be someone else online.
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh.

SECTION 66E — VIOLATION OF PRIVACY:
Intentionally or knowingly capturing, publishing, or transmitting images
of someone's private parts without consent (voyeurism).
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine up to Rs. 2 lakh OR BOTH.

SECTION 66F — CYBER TERRORISM:
Accessing computer resources, denying access, or introducing viruses
to threaten the unity, integrity, security or sovereignty of India.
PUNISHMENT: Life imprisonment.

SECTION 67 — PUBLISHING OBSCENE MATERIAL ONLINE:
Publishing or transmitting obscene material in electronic form.
PUNISHMENT: First conviction: Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine up to Rs. 5 lakh.
Subsequent conviction: Imprisonment up to 5 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakh.

SECTION 67A — PUBLISHING SEXUALLY EXPLICIT MATERIAL:
Publishing material containing sexually explicit acts.
PUNISHMENT: First conviction: Imprisonment up to 5 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakh.
Subsequent conviction: Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakh.

SECTION 67B — CHILD PORNOGRAPHY:
Publishing, browsing, or downloading child pornography (sexually explicit
material involving children).
PUNISHMENT: First conviction: Imprisonment up to 5 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakh.
Subsequent conviction: Imprisonment up to 7 years + Fine up to Rs. 10 lakh.

SECTION 72 — BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY AND PRIVACY:
Disclosing confidential information obtained in the course of duties
without the person's consent.
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 2 years OR Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh OR BOTH.

SECTION 72A — DISCLOSURE OF INFORMATION IN BREACH OF LAWFUL CONTRACT:
Service providers and intermediaries disclosing personal information.
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 3 years OR Fine up to Rs. 5 lakh OR BOTH.

SECTION 75 — OFFENCES COMMITTED OUTSIDE INDIA:
The IT Act applies to offences committed outside India if the computer or
server used is located in India. India has extra-territorial jurisdiction.

PART 2: CYBER BULLYING AND ONLINE HARASSMENT
=============================================

Cyberbullying has no single dedicated law but is covered by multiple laws:

FOR ONLINE THREATS AND INTIMIDATION:
- IT Act Section 66 (computer offences)
- IPC/BNS Section 503 — Criminal intimidation
- IPC/BNS Section 506 — Punishment for criminal intimidation

FOR ONLINE STALKING:
- BNS Section 78 — Stalking (follows, contacts, monitors person online)
  Punishment: First offence: Imprisonment up to 3 years + Fine
  Subsequent offence: Imprisonment up to 5 years + Fine

FOR SENDING OBSCENE/OFFENSIVE MESSAGES:
- IPC Section 354C — Voyeurism (capturing and circulating intimate images)
- IPC Section 509 — Words, gestures insulting modesty of woman
- BNS equivalent sections apply similarly

FOR FAKE PROFILES / IMPERSONATION:
- IT Act Section 66D — Cheating by personation
- IPC Section 419 — Cheating by personation

FOR REVENGE PORN / NON-CONSENSUAL INTIMATE IMAGES:
- IT Act Section 66E — Violation of privacy
- IT Act Section 67 — Obscene material
- IPC Section 354C — Voyeurism
Multiple punishments can run simultaneously.

CYBERBULLYING IN SCHOOLS AND COLLEGES:
- UGC Regulations apply
- School management can take disciplinary action
- POCSO Act applies if victim is below 18 years

HOW TO REPORT CYBERBULLYING:
1. National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: www.cybercrime.gov.in
2. Cybercrime helpline: 1930
3. Local Police Cyber Cell
4. Report to platform (social media's reporting mechanism)

PART 3: ONLINE FRAUD AND FINANCIAL CYBER CRIMES
================================================

TYPES OF ONLINE FRAUD:
1. Phishing: Fake emails/websites to steal passwords/bank details
2. Vishing: Phone calls to steal OTP/bank details
3. Smishing: SMS fraud — fake bank messages with malicious links
4. UPI Fraud: Fake payment requests, QR code fraud
5. Job Fraud: Fake job offers asking for money
6. Lottery Fraud: You've won a lottery you never entered
7. Investment Fraud: Fake investment schemes promising high returns
8. Sextortion: Threatening to release intimate images unless paid
9. SIM Swap Fraud: Taking over your mobile number to access bank accounts
10. Online Shopping Fraud: Non-delivery of goods ordered online

APPLICABLE LAWS:
- IT Act Section 66C — Identity theft
- IT Act Section 66D — Cheating by personation
- IPC/BNS Section 420 — Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property
- IPC/BNS Section 415-424 — Various cheating offences

IMMEDIATE STEPS IF DEFRAUDED ONLINE:
1. CALL HELPLINE 1930 IMMEDIATELY — Within 1-2 hours for best chance of freezing money
2. Report at: www.cybercrime.gov.in
3. Lodge complaint at nearest cyber police station
4. Preserve all evidence: screenshots, call recordings, transaction IDs
5. Contact your bank immediately to report fraud and block card

UPI/BANK FRAUD — ADDITIONAL STEPS:
- Call your bank's 24-hour helpline immediately
- Apply for chargeback if payment was made by card
- Reserve Bank of India (RBI) complaint: www.rbi.org.in > Complaints > SACHET

PART 4: SOCIAL MEDIA RELATED LAWS
===================================

WHAT CAN LAND YOU IN TROUBLE ONLINE:

1. DEFAMATION: Posting false statements that harm someone's reputation.
   - IPC Section 499/500 — Criminal defamation: Imprisonment up to 2 years
   - Civil defamation: Compensation in civil courts

2. HATE SPEECH: Promoting enmity between communities online.
   - IPC/BNS Section 153A — Promoting enmity: Imprisonment up to 3 years
   - Section 295A — Deliberate acts to outrage religious feelings

3. FAKE NEWS: Spreading false information can attract:
   - IPC Section 505 — Statements conducing to public mischief
   - Disaster Management Act 2005 (during disasters/pandemic)

4. COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT: Sharing copyrighted content without permission.
   - Copyright Act 1957 — Civil and criminal penalties

5. TROLLING/HARASSMENT:
   - If targeted at a specific person, can be criminal intimidation or harassment

YOUR RIGHTS AGAINST SOCIAL MEDIA PLATFORMS:
- Under IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules 2021:
  - Significant Social Media Intermediaries (>50 lakh users) must appoint:
    - Grievance Officer (Indian resident)
    - Nodal Contact Person
    - Chief Compliance Officer
  - Must acknowledge complaints within 24 hours
  - Resolve complaints within 15 days

HOW TO ESCALATE SOCIAL MEDIA COMPLAINTS:
1. First: Report to platform using built-in reporting tool
2. Next: Contact platform's Grievance Officer
3. Escalate: Appellate Committee (IT Rules 2021) — only if platform doesn't resolve
4. Additionally: Complaint at cybercrime.gov.in or cyber police station

PART 5: DATA PRIVACY IN INDIA
==============================

DIGITAL PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION ACT, 2023 (DPDPA):
India's new data protection law — largely not yet in force (rules being drafted).

KEY PRINCIPLES:
1. Consent-based data processing: Companies must get your consent before
   collecting personal data
2. Purpose limitation: Data can only be used for the stated purpose
3. Data minimization: Only necessary data should be collected
4. Right to Erasure: You can ask companies to delete your data
5. Right to Grievance: Complaint mechanism with Data Protection Board

YOUR RIGHTS UNDER DPDPA:
- Right to access: Know what data is collected about you
- Right to correction: Correct inaccurate data
- Right to erasure: Delete your data (Right to be Forgotten)
- Right to grievance: Complain about data processing
- Right to nominate: Nominate someone to exercise rights on your behalf

DATA FIDUCIARY OBLIGATIONS:
- Must maintain reasonable security safeguards
- Notify users of data breaches promptly
- Must publish contact details of Data Protection Officer

PENALTIES (When enforcement begins):
- Breach of child's data: Up to Rs. 200 crore
- Failure to notify breach: Up to Rs. 200 crore
- Inadequate security measures: Up to Rs. 250 crore
- General violations: Up to Rs. 50 crore

PART 6: CYBER CRIME HELPLINES AND RESOURCES
============================================

NATIONAL CYBERCRIME REPORTING PORTAL:
Website: www.cybercrime.gov.in
Phone Helpline: 1930 (24x7)

WHAT CAN BE REPORTED:
- Online financial fraud
- Online and social media related crimes
- Crimes against children online (CSAM)
- Cyber stalking and bullying
- Ransomware and data theft
- Any other cyber crime

DOCUMENTS TO KEEP READY WHEN FILING COMPLAINT:
- Screenshots of all relevant conversations/posts
- URLs of websites/profiles involved
- Transaction IDs/UTR numbers for financial fraud
- Email headers (for phishing emails)
- Your phone number and affected account details
- Time and date of incident

CYBER FORENSICS:
- Police have Cyber Forensic Labs to recover digital evidence
- Do NOT delete any evidence from your device
- Do NOT try to reformat or factory reset your device
- Report as soon as possible — digital evidence degrades

STATE CYBER CELLS:
Every state has a Cyber Crime Investigation Cell/Unit.
Contact your state police website for local cyber cell contact details.

INTERPOL AND FOREIGN JURISDICTION CRIMES:
For crimes originating from outside India:
- Report to CBI's Interpol wing if international coordination is needed
- CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team): www.cert-in.org.in
  For reporting large-scale cyber attacks on critical infrastructure.
