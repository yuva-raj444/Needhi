GOVERNMENT WELFARE SCHEMES AND CITIZEN ENTITLEMENTS — INDIA

PRADHAN MANTRI JAN DHAN YOJANA (PMJDY):
- Zero balance savings bank account for every household
- RuPay Debit Card
- Accidental insurance cover of Rs 2 lakh
- Life insurance cover of Rs 30,000
- Overdraft facility up to Rs 10,000
- Eligibility: Any Indian citizen above 10 years

PRADHAN MANTRI JEEVAN JYOTI BIMA YOJANA (PMJJBY):
- Life insurance of Rs 2 lakh
- Premium: Rs 436 per year
- Age group: 18-55 years
- Renewable every year
- Death benefit payable to nominee

PRADHAN MANTRI SURAKSHA BIMA YOJANA (PMSBY):
- Accidental death and disability insurance
- Premium: Rs 20 per year
- Accidental death: Rs 2 lakh
- Total permanent disability: Rs 2 lakh
- Partial permanent disability: Rs 1 lakh
- Age group: 18-70 years

ATAL PENSION YOJANA (APY):
- Guaranteed pension from Rs 1,000 to Rs 5,000 per month after age 60
- Contribution varies by age of joining and pension amount chosen
- Age group: 18-40 years
- Government co-contributes 50% for those not covered under any statutory social security scheme (for 5 years)

PRADHAN MANTRI AWAS YOJANA (PMAY):
- Housing for All
- Urban (PMAY-U): Interest subsidy of 6.5% for EWS/LIG (loan up to Rs 6 lakh), 4% for MIG-I (loan up to Rs 9 lakh), 3% for MIG-II (loan up to Rs 12 lakh)
- Rural (PMAY-G): Rs 1.20 lakh in plains, Rs 1.30 lakh in hilly/difficult areas
- Eligibility: No pucca house, priority for women ownership

MAHATMA GANDHI NATIONAL RURAL EMPLOYMENT GUARANTEE ACT (MGNREGA), 2005:
- Guarantees 100 days of wage employment per year to every rural household
- Demand-driven — employment must be provided within 15 days of application
- Unemployment allowance if work not provided within 15 days
- Minimum wage rate as notified by the State Government
- Work must be within 5 km of applicant's residence
- Women must constitute at least 1/3 of beneficiaries
- Payment within 15 days of work done
- Social audit by Gram Sabha
- No contractor or machinery — only manual labour

PM KISAN SAMMAN NIDHI:
- Rs 6,000 per year in three equal instalments of Rs 2,000 each
- For all landholding farmer families
- Directly transferred to bank account (DBT)
- Eligibility: All farmer families with cultivable land (exclusions: institutional landholders, income tax payers, government employees)

FOOD SECURITY — NATIONAL FOOD SECURITY ACT, 2013:
- Provides subsidized foodgrains to approximately 2/3 of India's population
- Entitled quantity: 5 kg per person per month for priority households
- Price: Rice at Rs 3/kg, wheat at Rs 2/kg, coarse grains at Rs 1/kg
- Antyodaya (poorest) families: 35 kg per household per month
- Maternity benefit: Rs 6,000 for pregnant and lactating mothers
- Children aged 6 months to 14 years: Free meals through schools and anganwadis
- Complaint mechanism through State Food Commission

AYUSHMAN BHARAT — PRADHAN MANTRI JAN AROGYA YOJANA (PM-JAY):
- Health insurance of Rs 5 lakh per family per year
- Covers secondary and tertiary care hospitalization
- Cashless treatment at empanelled hospitals
- No restriction on family size, age, gender
- Pre-existing diseases covered from day one
- Covers 3 days of pre-hospitalization and 15 days of post-hospitalization expenses
- Eligibility: Based on SECC (Socio-Economic Caste Census) data — targeted at bottom 40% of population

PRADHAN MANTRI MATRU VANDANA YOJANA (PMMVY):
- Rs 5,000 to pregnant and lactating mothers for first living child
- Direct benefit transfer in three instalments
- Eligibility: All pregnant women and lactating mothers for first child

SUKANYA SAMRIDDHI YOJANA:
- Savings scheme for girl child
- Account can be opened for girl child below 10 years
- Minimum deposit: Rs 250 per year, Maximum: Rs 1.5 lakh per year
- Maturity: 21 years from date of opening or marriage after 18
- Interest rate: Government-decided (currently around 8%)
- Tax benefits under Section 80C

STAND UP INDIA:
- Loans from Rs 10 lakh to Rs 1 crore
- For SC, ST and Women entrepreneurs
- For setting up greenfield enterprises in manufacturing, services, or trading
- At least one SC/ST and one woman borrower per bank branch

MUDRA YOJANA:
- Loans up to Rs 10 lakh for micro-enterprises
- Three categories: Shishu (up to Rs 50,000), Kishore (Rs 50,000 to Rs 5 lakh), Tarun (Rs 5 lakh to Rs 10 lakh)
- No collateral required
- Available through all commercial banks, RRBs, small finance banks, and MFIs

LEGAL AID — LEGAL SERVICES AUTHORITIES ACT, 1987

SECTION 12 — CRITERIA FOR GIVING LEGAL SERVICES
Every person who has to file or defend a case shall be entitled to legal services if that person is:
(a) a member of Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe;
(b) a victim of trafficking or begar;
(c) a woman or a child;
(d) a mentally ill or otherwise disabled person;
(e) a person under circumstances of undeserved want (income less than Rs 9,000 per month for Supreme Court matters, or Rs 12,000 for other matters — varies by state);
(f) an industrial workman;
(g) a person in custody;
(h) a person in receipt of annual income less than the prescribed amount.

SECTION 39A — CONSTITUTION RIGHT TO FREE LEGAL AID
Article 39A of the Constitution directs the State to secure that the operation of the legal system promotes justice on a basis of equal opportunity and provides free legal aid.

HOW TO ACCESS FREE LEGAL AID:
1. Apply to the District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) in your district
2. Apply to the Taluka Legal Services Committee
3. Apply to the State Legal Services Authority
4. Apply to the National Legal Services Authority (NALSA)
5. Visit nearest legal aid clinic (often in courts)
6. Call NALSA helpline

CONSUMER DISPUTES REDRESSAL — COMPLETE GUIDE

WHERE TO FILE COMPLAINTS:
1. District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: Goods/services value up to Rs 1 crore
2. State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: Value above Rs 1 crore but up to Rs 10 crore
3. National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: Value above Rs 10 crore

HOW TO FILE:
1. File a complaint in writing or electronically (e-daakhil.nic.in)
2. Pay prescribed fee (Rs 100 for claims up to Rs 5 lakh, increasing with claim amount)
3. Attach supporting documents (bills, receipts, warranty cards, correspondence)
4. Filed within 2 years of cause of action (can be condoned for sufficient cause)

WHAT CAN BE CLAIMED:
1. Replacement of defective goods
2. Removal of defect in goods/service
3. Refund of price paid
4. Compensation for loss or injury suffered
5. Punitive damages
6. Cost of complaint and proceedings
7. Discontinuance of unfair trade practice
8. Withdrawal of hazardous goods from sale

REMEDIES AVAILABLE:
The Commission can order:
(a) Remove the defect from goods or deficiency in service
(b) Replace the goods
(c) Return the price
(d) Pay compensation for any loss or injury
(e) Discontinue the unfair trade practice
(f) Not to offer hazardous goods for sale
(g) Pay adequate costs
(h) Pay punitive damages

APPEAL:
From District Commission → State Commission (within 45 days)
From State Commission → National Commission (within 30 days)
From National Commission → Supreme Court (within 30 days)

IMPORTANT: The Consumer Protection Act 2019 now also covers E-COMMERCE transactions. Online marketplaces and sellers are liable for product quality and service delivery.
