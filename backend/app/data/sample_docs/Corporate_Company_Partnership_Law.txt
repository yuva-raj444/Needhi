CORPORATE AND COMPANY LAW IN INDIA — BUSINESSES, DIRECTORS AND SHAREHOLDERS
=============================================================================

PART 1: COMPANIES ACT, 2013 — OVERVIEW
========================================

The Companies Act 2013 governs the formation, regulation, and winding up of companies in India.
Administered by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA).
MCA Portal: www.mca.gov.in

TYPES OF COMPANIES:

1. PRIVATE LIMITED COMPANY:
   - Minimum 2 shareholders, Maximum 200 shareholders
   - Minimum 2 directors (1 must be Indian resident)
   - "Pvt. Ltd." in name
   - Cannot raise money from public
   - Most common business structure in India

2. PUBLIC LIMITED COMPANY:
   - Minimum 7 shareholders, no maximum limit
   - Minimum 3 directors
   - "Ltd." in name
   - Can raise money from public (IPO)

3. ONE PERSON COMPANY (OPC):
   - Single shareholder/director
   - Limited liability protection
   - Must convert when turnover exceeds Rs. 2 crore

4. LIMITED LIABILITY PARTNERSHIP (LLP):
   - Governed by LLP Act 2008
   - Partners have limited liability
   - No minimum capital requirement

5. SECTION 8 COMPANY (NON-PROFIT):
   - For charitable/religious/educational purposes
   - Profits reinvested, not distributed

6. PRODUCER COMPANY:
   - For farmers, artisans, and producers
   - Governed by Companies Act but specific sections apply

PART 2: INCORPORATION OF A COMPANY
=====================================

STEPS TO REGISTER A COMPANY:
1. Obtain Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) for directors
2. Apply for Director Identification Number (DIN) on MCA portal
3. Reserve company name through MCA portal (RUN form)
4. File SPICe+ form (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically)
5. Memorandum of Association (MoA) and Articles of Association (AoA) drafted
6. Certificate of Incorporation issued by Registrar of Companies (ROC)

MANDATORY POST-INCORPORATION STEPS:
- PAN and TAN registration
- GST registration (if turnover threshold crossed)
- Bank account opening
- Shops and Establishments Act registration
- Professional Tax registration (state-specific)
- EPFO/ESIC registration (when employees join)

MEMORANDUM OF ASSOCIATION (MoA):
The constitution of the company — defines the company's objects/purpose.
A company can only do activities mentioned in its MoA.

ARTICLES OF ASSOCIATION (AoA):
Internal rules and regulations of the company — how it is managed.

PART 3: SHAREHOLDERS' RIGHTS
==============================

BASIC RIGHTS OF SHAREHOLDERS:
1. Right to vote at general meetings (one share = one vote typically)
2. Right to receive dividends when declared
3. Right to attend Annual General Meeting (AGM)
4. Right to inspect company books and annual report
5. Right to transfer shares (subject to Articles of Association)
6. Right to receive notice of meetings
7. Right to appoint proxy to vote on your behalf

ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING (AGM):
- Must be held every year within 6 months of financial year end
- Within 9 months for first AGM
- 21 days notice must be given to all shareholders

EXTRAORDINARY GENERAL MEETING (EGM):
Called for special resolutions — merger, major policy changes, etc.

OPPRESSION AND MISMANAGEMENT (Sections 241-246):
If majority shareholders oppress minority shareholders, the minority can:
- Apply to National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) for relief
- NCLT can regulate the affairs of the company

PART 4: DIRECTOR'S DUTIES AND LIABILITIES
===========================================

DUTIES OF DIRECTORS (Section 166):
1. Act in accordance with the Articles
2. Act in good faith for the benefit of company and shareholders
3. Exercise independent judgment
4. Exercise reasonable care, skill, and diligence
5. Avoid conflicts of interest
6. Not accept benefits from third parties
7. Declare interest in related party transactions

DISQUALIFICATION OF DIRECTORS (Section 164):
A person cannot be a director if:
- Declared of unsound mind by court
- Insolvent / adjudicated bankrupt
- Convicted of offence involving moral turpitude (5+ years)
- Final order of conviction for offence punishable with 6 months imprisonment
- Company has not filed annual returns for 3 consecutive years

DIRECTOR IDENTIFICATION NUMBER (DIN):
Unique number allotted to each director.
Mandatory for all directors of Indian companies.

INDEPENDENT DIRECTORS:
- Listed companies must have 1/3 independent directors
- No material financial relationship with company
- Maximum tenure: 2 terms of 5 years each

PART 5: COMPANY ACCOUNTS AND AUDIT
=====================================

FINANCIAL YEAR:
Companies must follow April 1 to March 31 as financial year.

ANNUAL RETURN:
Every company must file Annual Return (MGT-7) with ROC within 60 days of AGM.

FINANCIAL STATEMENTS:
Must be filed with ROC (Form AOC-4) within 30 days of AGM.

STATUTORY AUDIT:
Mandatory for all companies. Auditor appointed at each AGM.

CORPORATE SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY (CSR):
Section 135: Companies with:
- Net worth Rs. 500 crore or more, OR
- Turnover Rs. 1,000 crore or more, OR
- Net profit Rs. 5 crore or more
Must spend 2% of average net profit of last 3 years on CSR activities.

PART 6: MERGERS AND ACQUISITIONS
===================================

MERGER / AMALGAMATION (Sections 230-232):
- Application to NCLT for approval
- Shareholders and creditors must approve
- Tax implications need careful planning

TAKEOVER:
SEBI Takeover Code (for listed companies):
- If acquiring 25%+ shares, must make Open Offer to public shareholders (for another 26%)
- Protects minority shareholders from forced buyout at low price

INSOLVENCY AND BANKRUPTCY CODE (IBC), 2016:
- For companies that cannot pay debts
- Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP)
- Application to NCLT by creditors or debtors
- 180 days + 90 days extension for resolution
- If no resolution: Liquidation
- IBBI (Insolvency and Bankruptcy Board of India): www.ibbi.gov.in

PART 7: PARTNERSHIP FIRMS
============================

INDIAN PARTNERSHIP ACT, 1932:
Traditional partnership structure (unlimited liability).

KEY FEATURES:
- Minimum 2 partners, maximum 50 partners (banking: 10 partners)
- Registration optional but advisable (unregistered partnerships
  cannot file suits in court for enforcing their rights)
- Partners are jointly and severally liable for firm's debts (UNLIMITED liability)
- Partnership deed should specify: profit sharing ratio, duties, dissolution

DISSOLUTION OF PARTNERSHIP:
- By agreement
- By court order (misconduct, persistent breach, mental incapacity)
- By insolvency
- By notice (for at-will partnerships)

LLP vs PARTNERSHIP FIRM:
LLP: Limited liability, separate legal entity, more compliance.
Firm: Unlimited liability, no separate legal entity, simpler structure.

PART 8: STARTUP INDIA
========================

STARTUP INDIA (Government scheme):
Website: startupindia.gov.in

ELIGIBILITY:
- Incorporated less than 10 years ago
- Annual turnover less than Rs. 100 crore in any year
- Working towards innovation/improvement of products or services
- Not formed by splitting or restructuring an existing business

BENEFITS:
1. Income tax exemption for 3 consecutive years in first 10 years
2. Exemption from Capital Gains Tax on investments
3. Self-certification compliance (Labour and Environment)
4. Fast-track patent examination at 80% reduced fee
5. Easy winding up (30 days under IBC)
6. Access to Fund of Funds corpus

HOW TO REGISTER:
Online application at: startupindia.gov.in
Recognition by DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade).

PART 9: COMPETITION LAW
=========================

COMPETITION ACT, 2002:
Prevents anti-competitive practices.
Administered by Competition Commission of India (CCI).
Website: cci.gov.in | Helpline: 011-46218400

PROHIBITED PRACTICES:
1. Agreements reducing competition:
   - Price fixing (cartels)
   - Market sharing
   - Bid rigging
   All "void" under the Act.

2. Abuse of Dominant Position:
   Dominant company cannot:
   - Impose unfair prices or conditions
   - Limit supply to harm consumers
   - Deny market access to competitors
   - Use predatory pricing (selling below cost to eliminate competitors)

3. Combinations (Mergers exceeding threshold):
   Must be notified to CCI before completion.
   CCI reviews impact on competition.

PENALTIES:
- Anti-competitive agreements: Up to 10% of average annual turnover, or 3 times profit
- Abuse of dominance: Up to 10% of annual turnover
- Failure to notify combination: Up to 1% of combined turnover

HOW TO FILE COMPLAINT WITH CCI:
Online: cci.gov.in > For Consumers > File Information
Or write to Director General, CCI, New Delhi.
