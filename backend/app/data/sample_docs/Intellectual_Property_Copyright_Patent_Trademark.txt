INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS IN INDIA — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
=============================================================

PART 1: COPYRIGHT LAW IN INDIA
================================

COPYRIGHT ACT, 1957:
Protects original literary, artistic, musical, and dramatic works.
Administered by the Copyright Office, Ministry of Commerce and Industry.
Website: copyright.gov.in

WHAT IS PROTECTED BY COPYRIGHT:
- Literary works: Books, articles, poems, computer programs, databases
- Dramatic works: Plays, dance choreography
- Musical works: Songs, compositions
- Artistic works: Paintings, sculptures, drawings, photographs
- Cinematograph films: Movies, videos
- Sound recordings

WHAT IS NOT PROTECTED:
- Ideas, concepts, facts, procedures, methods (only expression is protected)
- Titles of books/songs (not protected by copyright but may be protected as trademark)
- News reports (facts reported, but not their creative expression)

WHEN DOES COPYRIGHT ARISE:
AUTOMATICALLY — as soon as the work is created and expressed.
No registration required for copyright to exist.
Registration is optional but creates evidence of authorship.

DURATION OF COPYRIGHT:
- Literary, dramatic, musical, artistic works: 60 years after author's death
- Cinematograph films: 60 years from publication
- Sound recordings: 60 years from publication
- Government works: 60 years from publication
- Anonymous works: 60 years from publication

WHO OWNS COPYRIGHT:
- Author/creator of the work
- For work created in employment: Employer owns copyright
- For films: Producer owns copyright
- For sound recordings: Producer owns copyright

RIGHTS OF COPYRIGHT HOLDER:
1. Reproduction rights
2. Translation rights
3. Public performance rights
4. Broadcasting rights
5. Adaptation rights
6. Distribution rights (Right to issue copies to public)

INFRINGEMENT:
Any act within the exclusive rights of copyright holder done without permission.
Exceptions (Fair Dealing):
- Private study or research
- Criticism or review
- News reporting
- Teaching in schools/colleges
- Reporting of court proceedings

PENALTIES FOR COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT:
- Civil: Injunction + damages
- Criminal: Imprisonment 6 months to 3 years + fine Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 2 lakh
  For repeat offenders or commercial scale: Imprisonment 1-3 years + higher fines

COMPLAINT FOR COPYRIGHT VIOLATION:
- Civil suit in District Court / High Court (if Delhi, can file in Delhi HC directly)
- Police complaint (FIR) for criminal offringement
- Online complaint to copyright.gov.in

PART 2: PATENT LAW IN INDIA
=============================

PATENTS ACT, 1970 (AMENDED 2005):
Grants exclusive rights to inventors.
Administered by the Office of the Controller General of Patents, Designs and Trade Marks.
Website: ipindia.gov.in

WHAT CAN BE PATENTED (SECTION 2):
- New invention
- Involving inventive step (not obvious to skilled person)
- Capable of industrial application

WHAT CANNOT BE PATENTED IN INDIA (SECTION 3):
- Abstract scientific theories or discoveries
- Mathematical methods
- Mental acts or business methods (in abstract form)
- Mere discovery of existing substance (no new use)
- Section 3(d): New form/use of known substance showing SAME therapeutic efficacy
  (Important: prevents evergreening of pharmaceutical patents)
- Plants, animals, biological processes
- Computer programs per se (software as such)

DURATION:
20 years from the date of filing.

HOW TO APPLY FOR PATENT:
Online at ipindia.gov.in or physical application at Patent Office (Delhi, Mumbai,
Kolkata, Chennai).

PATENT PROCESS:
1. File provisional or complete specification
2. Publication after 18 months
3. Request for examination
4. Office actions and responses
5. Grant of patent if all conditions met

COMPULSORY LICENSING:
Government can grant compulsory license to manufacture patented drugs/products
if public health requires it (Natco Pharma case — Nexavar cancer drug).

PART 3: TRADEMARK LAW
======================

TRADE MARKS ACT, 1999:
Protects distinctive marks that identify goods/services.
Website: ipindia.gov.in

WHAT CAN BE REGISTERED AS TRADEMARK:
- Words, logos, numerals, shapes, colors, sounds, smells
- Combination of these
- Must be distinctive — able to distinguish goods/services from others

WHAT CANNOT BE REGISTERED:
- Descriptive marks (just describing the product)
- Generic words
- Marks deceptively similar to existing marks
- Geographic names (unless distinctive)
- Marks offensive to public morality

DURATION:
10 years, renewable indefinitely every 10 years (on payment of fee).

HOW TO APPLY:
Online at ipindiaservices.gov.in
Classes: International Classification System — 45 classes (1-34 goods, 35-45 services)

TM vs ® SYMBOL:
TM: Trademark application filed — claim made but not yet registered
®: Registered trademark — legal registration complete

TRADEMARK INFRINGEMENT:
Using a registered trademark without permission.
Passing off: Misrepresenting goods as those of another (even unregistered marks).

REMEDIES:
- Civil: Injunction, damages, account of profits
- Criminal: Imprisonment up to 3 years + fine Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 2 lakh
- Also: Customs can seize goods with infringing marks

PART 4: GEOGRAPHICAL INDICATIONS (GI TAGS)
============================================

GEOGRAPHICAL INDICATIONS OF GOODS ACT, 1999:
Protects products with specific geographic origin and qualities.

FAMOUS INDIAN GI TAGS:
- Darjeeling Tea
- Kanchipuram Silk
- Banaras Brocades and Sarees
- Alphonso Mangoes (Ratnagiri)
- Mysore Silk
- Kolhapuri Chappal
- Chanderi Sarees
- Kashmir Pashmina
- Tirupathi Laddu
- Basmati Rice

BENEFIT:
Producers in the designated geographic area can use the GI tag.
Prevents misuse of the name by others.

PART 5: INDUSTRIAL DESIGN
===========================

DESIGNS ACT, 2000:
Protects the visual design of an object (aesthetic features, not function).
Duration: 10 years (extendable by 5 more years).

Registration at the same office as patents (ipindia.gov.in).

PART 6: TRADE SECRETS AND CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
====================================================

India does not have a dedicated Trade Secrets law.
Protected through:
- Contract law (confidentiality agreements / NDAs)
- Law of breach of confidence (common law)
- Information Technology Act (for digital confidential data)

NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT (NDA):
Legal contract to keep information confidential.
Enforceable in Indian courts.
Breach of NDA: Civil damages and injunction.

PART 7: IP ENFORCEMENT — WHERE TO GO
======================================

1. CIVIL COURTS:
   - District Court (for all IP matters)
   - High Court — directly for trademarks, patents (if value is high)
   - Delhi High Court has special IP jurisdiction for certain registered
     trademarks/patents

2. POLICE/FIR:
   For criminal IP infringement (copying, piracy, counterfeiting)
   File FIR at local police station.
   Cyber crime cell for online piracy.

3. CUSTOMS:
   Customs Act allows seizure of infringing goods at borders.
   Register mark/copyright with Customs for automatic border protection.

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY APPELLATE BOARD (IPAB):
   Abolished in 2021.
   Appeals now go directly to High Courts.

5. ONLINE PIRACY:
   IT Act applies to digital piracy.
   Report to the platform (YouTube DMCA notices, etc.)
   File complaint at cybercrime.gov.in
