PART 2: LIFE INSURANCE
========================

WHAT IS LIFE INSURANCE:
Financial protection for family in case of policyholder's death.

TYPES:
1. Term Insurance: Pure death benefit, no maturity benefit
2. Endowment: Death benefit + savings component
3. ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plan): Insurance + investment
4. Whole Life: Coverage for entire life
5. Money-back: Periodic survival benefits

YOUR RIGHTS AS A POLICYHOLDER:

1. FREE LOOK PERIOD:
   You can cancel any new insurance policy within 30 days of receiving it
   WITHOUT any penalty and get full refund (minus risk premium for coverage period).
   This is your right — no company can deny this.

2. RIGHT AGAINST MISSELLING:
   If a policy was sold to you by providing wrong information or pressure,
   you can file complaint with IRDAI.

3. RIGHT TO CLAIM SETTLEMENT:
   Life insurance claim must be settled within 30 days of receiving
   all documents. If investigation needed: 90 days maximum.

4. NOMINATION:
   You must nominate someone to receive the claim. Can be changed anytime.
   Nominees should keep the policy documents safely.

CLAIM REJECTION:
If claim is rejected:
- Ask for written reasons for rejection
- If reason is incorrect/unjustified:
  1. Complaint to insurance company's Grievance Cell
  2. Complaint to Insurance Ombudsman (within 1 year of rejection)
  3. Consumer Forum
  4. Civil Court

INSURANCE OMBUDSMAN:
Available in all major cities. FREE and QUICK resolution.
Website: ecoi.co.in | Helpline: 155255

PART 3: HEALTH INSURANCE
==========================

KEY HEALTH INSURANCE TERMS:

1. SUM INSURED: Maximum coverage amount per year
2. PREMIUM: Annual payment for the policy
3. WAITING PERIOD: Time before certain conditions are covered
   - Initial waiting period: Usually 30 days (except accidents)
   - Pre-existing disease waiting period: Usually 2-4 years
4. CASHLESS FACILITY: Hospital charges directly settled with insurer
5. REIMBURSEMENT: Pay first, then claim from insurer
6. CO-PAY: % of claim you must pay (e.g., 10% co-payment)
7. SUB-LIMIT: Cap on specific treatments (e.g., room rent limits)
8. EXCLUSIONS: What the policy does NOT cover

MANDATORY HEALTH INSURANCE PORTABILITY:
You can switch health insurer without losing benefits like accumulated
no-claim bonus or pre-existing disease waiting period credit.
Inform new insurer at least 45 days before policy renewal.

INSURER CANNOT REJECT RENEWAL:
Once you have a policy for 8 continuous years, insurer cannot reject
renewal based on claims made (principle of non-forfeiture).

PRE-EXISTING DISEASE:
After 4 years of continuous coverage (sometimes 2 years), pre-existing
diseases must be covered by insurer.

CLAIM REJECTION GROUNDS (Common):
- Non-disclosure of pre-existing condition (READ FORM CAREFULLY before signing)
- Treatment not covered
- Waiting period not over
- Policy lapsed due to non-payment

HOW TO CONTEST REJECTION:
1. Internal grievance redressal of the insurer (respond within 15 days)
2. IRDAI Bima Bharosa portal: bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in
3. Insurance Ombudsman: ecoi.co.in
4. Consumer Forum (FOMCA)

AYUSHMAN BHARAT:
Government's flagship health insurance scheme.
Free hospitalization up to Rs. 5 lakh per year for eligible families.
Helpline: 14555 | Website: pmjay.gov.in

PART 4: MOTOR ACCIDENT CLAIMS
===============================

MOTOR VEHICLES ACT, 1988:
All motor vehicles must have THIRD PARTY liability insurance by law.
Driving without valid third-party insurance: Fine Rs. 2,000 (first offence).

MOTOR ACCIDENTS CLAIMS TRIBUNAL (MACT):
Dedicated tribunal for settling accident compensation claims.
Established under Motor Vehicles Act.

WHO CAN CLAIM:
- Accident victim (for injury)
- Family members (for death)
- Property owner (for property damage)

THIRD PARTY CLAIM PROCESS:
1. FIR must be lodged at nearest police station
2. File claim petition at MACT (Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal)
3. Notice is served on insurance company and vehicle owner
4. Evidence: FIR, medical bills, death certificate, post-mortem report
5. Award by Tribunal

SECOND SCHEDULE — COMPENSATION FOR DEATH:
Multiplier method: Monthly income × Multiplier (based on age) = Compensation
Dependency deduction: 1/3rd if one earner and 2 dependents
Additional heads: Funeral expenses, loss of estate, pain and suffering

INTERIM RELIEF:
Tribunal can award 50% of likely compensation as interim (preliminary)
payment to help victims while case is pending.

NO-FAULT LIABILITY (Section 140 MV Act):
Accident victims can get quick compensation without proving fault:
- Death: Rs. 50,000
- Grievous injury: Rs. 25,000

DRUNK DRIVING ACCIDENTS:
Insurance company CAN recover amount paid from the driver if they were drunk.
Drunk driving is a criminal offence — separate proceedings under IPC/BNS.

PART 6: CHEQUE BOUNCE (NI ACT SECTION 138)
============================================

When a cheque is dishonoured due to insufficient funds or for any other
reason (except signature mismatch or technical defect):

PROCEDURE:
1. Bank gives you "Cheque Return Memo" with reason
2. Send LEGAL NOTICE to drawer within 30 days of receiving memo
3. If no payment within 15 days of notice: FILE COMPLAINT in court
   within 30 days of expiry of 15-day notice period
4. Magistrate issues summons
5. If convicted: Imprisonment up to 2 years OR fine up to twice the cheque amount

LIMITATION: 
The entire process (notice + complaint) must start within 30 days of 
dishonour. Don't delay.

CHEQUE RETURN MEMO REASONS:
"Insufficient funds" — Section 138 NI Act applies — criminal remedy
"Account closed" — Section 138 applies
"Payment stopped" — In genuine disputes, civil remedy may be more appropriate

PART 7: INTERNET BANKING AND DIGITAL PAYMENT FRAUD
====================================================

RBI GUIDELINES ON ZERO LIABILITY:
If fraud happens WITHOUT your fault (phishing by bank, third party fraud
without you sharing OTP), you have ZERO LIABILITY — bank MUST reimburse.

IF FRAUD HAPPENED DUE TO YOUR NEGLIGENCE (sharing OTP, clicking phishing links):
You bear some or full liability.

REPORT WITHIN 3 WORKING DAYS: Zero liability.
REPORT WITHIN 4-7 DAYS: Limited liability (shared with bank).
REPORT AFTER 7 DAYS: Bank's policy.

IMMEDIATE ACTION ON UPI/BANKING FRAUD:
1. Call 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline) immediately
2. Call your bank's 24-hour helpline
3. Report at cybercrime.gov.in
4. Lodge complaint with bank in writing
5. File complaint at Banking Ombudsman if bank doesn't resolve in 30 days
