IMMIGRATION, CITIZENSHIP, PASSPORT AND NRI RIGHTS IN INDIA
============================================================

PART 1: CITIZENSHIP OF INDIA
==============================

CITIZENSHIP ACT, 1955:
Governs acquisition and loss of Indian citizenship.

WAYS TO ACQUIRE INDIAN CITIZENSHIP:

1. BY BIRTH:
   - Born in India on or after January 26, 1950 but before July 1, 1987: 
     Citizen by birth regardless of parents' nationality.
   - Born between July 1, 1987 and December 3, 2004:
     Citizen only if at least one parent is Indian citizen.
   - Born on or after December 4, 2004:
     Citizen only if BOTH parents are Indian citizens, OR one is Indian
     citizen and other is NOT an illegal migrant.

2. BY DESCENT:
   - Born outside India — if parent was Indian citizen at time of birth.
   - Must register at Indian consulate within 1 year.

3. BY REGISTRATION:
   - Person of Indian Origin (PIO) ordinarily resident for 7 years
   - Spouse of Indian citizen ordinarily resident for 7 years
   - Minors whose parents are citizens
   - Independent Commonwealth citizens of good character resident for 5 years

4. BY NATURALISATION:
   - Must have resided in India for 12 years (total), including last 12 months
   - Must be of good character
   - Must have adequate knowledge of one language from Schedule 8 of Constitution
   - Must renounce previous citizenship

5. OVERSEAS CITIZENS OF INDIA (OCI):
   - Not full citizenship — a form of permanent residency
   - No voting rights, no public service jobs, no agricultural land purchase
   - Lifelong visa to India, no need to register for any length of stay
   - Foreign nationals of Indian origin can apply

LOSS OF CITIZENSHIP:
- Voluntary renunciation
- Acquisition of foreign citizenship (auto-loss)
- Deprivation by government (disloyalty, fraud in obtaining citizenship)

CITIZENSHIP AMENDMENT ACT, 2019 (CAA):
Provides accelerated citizenship to persecuted minorities (Hindus, Sikhs, 
Buddhists, Jains, Parsis, Christians) from Pakistan, Bangladesh, and 
Afghanistan who entered India before December 31, 2014. 
Does NOT apply to Muslims from these countries.
Rules notified in 2024.

PART 2: PASSPORT AND TRAVEL DOCUMENTS
=======================================

PASSPORT ACT, 1967:
Governs issuance and impounding of passports.

TYPES OF PASSPORTS:
1. REGULAR (NAVY BLUE): For ordinary travel
2. OFFICIAL (WHITE): For government officials on duty
3. DIPLOMATIC (MAROON): For diplomats

WHO CAN GET A PASSPORT:
All Indian citizens are entitled to a passport unless:
- Criminal proceedings are pending
- Court order prohibiting departure
- Government has impounded passport

HOW TO APPLY FOR PASSPORT:
Online: passportindia.gov.in
Call Centre: 1800-258-1800

Steps:
1. Register on Passport Seva Portal
2. Fill application form
3. Pay fees online
4. Book appointment at Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) or Post Office PSK
5. Visit PSK with original documents
6. Police verification (post or pre-issuance depending on category)
7. Passport dispatched

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR FIRST PASSPORT:
- Proof of Date of Birth (Birth certificate, school leaving certificate)
- Proof of Address (Aadhaar, voter ID, bank passbook, electricity bill)
- Proof of Indian citizenship (if not evident from above)
- Old passport (if any)

TATKAL (URGENT) PASSPORT:
Available for urgent cases — higher fee.
Normal processing: 1-3 weeks.
Tatkal: 3-7 working days.

MINOR'S PASSPORT:
- Parents must apply on behalf of minors
- Both parents' signatures required (if both are alive)
- Short validity (5 years or until 18, whichever earlier)

IMPOUNDING OF PASSPORT:
Government can impound/revoke passport if:
- Criminal proceedings pending
- Required to attend court
- Person suspected of committing serious offence
- Not in public interest

Right to appeal against impounding: District Court.

PART 3: VISA AND FOREIGNER LAWS
=================================

FOREIGNERS ACT, 1946 and REGISTRATION OF FOREIGNERS ACT, 1939:
All foreigners must comply with their visa conditions.

TYPES OF VISAS FOR INDIA:
- Tourist Visa (TV): For tourism, visiting friends
- Business Visa (BV): For business activities
- Employment Visa (EV): For working in India
- Student Visa (SV): For studying
- Medical Visa (MV): For medical treatment
- Research Visa: For research activities
- e-Visa: Online visa for many nationalities for short stays

OVERSTAYING VISA:
- Serious offence under Foreigners Act
- Can lead to detention, deportation, ban on re-entry
- Must extend visa at FRRO (Foreigners Regional Registration Office) before expiry

FRRO OFFICES:
Present in major cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Bengaluru, Kolkata, Hyderabad)
Website: indianfrro.gov.in | Helpline: 011-26711384

FOREIGNER REGISTRATION:
Foreigners staying more than 180 days must register with FRRO.
Pakistani, Chinese, and certain other nationals must register within 14 days.

PART 4: NRI — NON-RESIDENT INDIAN RIGHTS
==========================================

WHO IS AN NRI:
An Indian citizen who resides outside India for more than 182 days in a
financial year. Tax status under FEMA and Income Tax Act.

BANKING AND FINANCE FOR NRIs:
1. NRE ACCOUNT (Non-Resident External):
   - For repatriating foreign earnings to India
   - Fully repatriable (can take money back abroad)
   - Interest tax-free in India

2. NRO ACCOUNT (Non-Resident Ordinary):
   - For income earned in India (rent, pension, etc.)
   - Repatriation limited to USD 1 million per year
   - Interest taxable in India

3. FCNR ACCOUNT (Foreign Currency Non-Resident):
   - Term deposits in foreign currency
   - Fully repatriable

NRI PROPERTY RIGHTS:
- NRIs can purchase residential and commercial property in India
- Agricultural land, plantation property, farmhouse — NRIs CANNOT purchase
  (unless inherited or received as gift)
- OCI cardholders have same property rights as NRIs

NRI INHERITANCE:
- NRIs can inherit any property in India (including agricultural land)
- Ancestral property rights same as resident Indians
- Will of Indian origin NRI executed abroad is valid in India

NRI MARRIAGES:
- Indian courts have jurisdiction over NRI marriages if married under Indian law
  or if either party is Indian citizen
- Mandatory registration of marriages involving NRIs (some states require)
- NRI Marriage Registration Form available at Indian consulates

NRI VOTING RIGHTS:
- NRIs who are Indian citizens can register as overseas voters
- Can vote in Lok Sabha and state assembly elections from abroad
- Registration at Indian Embassy/Consulate in country of residence

NRI GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL:
- Ministry of External Affairs: mea.gov.in/overseas-indians.htm
- Madad Portal (consular assistance): madad.gov.in
- NRI Helpline: 1800-11-3090 (toll free from India) / +91-11-26100844 (from abroad)
- Pravasi Bharatiya Kendra: New Delhi

PART 5: OCI — OVERSEAS CITIZEN OF INDIA
=========================================

WHO CAN APPLY FOR OCI:
- Former Indian citizens and their descendants (up to 4 generations)
- Spouse of Indian citizen or OCI holder (not Pakistani or Bangladeshi national)

BENEFITS OF OCI:
- Multiple entry lifelong visa to India
- No requirement to register with Foreigners Registration Office
- Parity with NRI in financial, economic and educational fields
- Can purchase non-agricultural immovable property
- Cannot hold some government jobs, cannot vote, cannot become President/Vice President

HOW TO APPLY:
Online at ociservices.gov.in through the nearest Indian Mission.

PART 6: EXTRADITION
====================

EXTRADITION ACT, 1962:
India can extradite persons to and from countries with bilateral treaties.

India has extradition treaties with major countries including UK, USA, 
Australia, France, Germany, Russia, and others.

EXTRADITION CANNOT HAPPEN IF:
- The offence is of a political character
- The person is a citizen of the country where they are
  (India generally does not extradite its own citizens)
- The statute of limitations has expired
- The person may face persecution based on race, religion, nationality, or politics
