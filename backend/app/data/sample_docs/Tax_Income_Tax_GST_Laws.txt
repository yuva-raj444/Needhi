TAX LAWS, INCOME TAX AND GST — CITIZEN'S GUIDE TO INDIAN TAX SYSTEM
======================================================================

PART 1: INCOME TAX — BASICS FOR CITIZENS
==========================================

INCOME TAX ACT, 1961:
Governs levy and collection of income tax in India.
Administered by Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT).

WHO MUST PAY INCOME TAX:
All residents of India (and non-residents for India-sourced income)
who have total income exceeding the basic exemption limit.

BASIC EXEMPTION LIMITS (AY 2024-25):
Under OLD TAX REGIME:
- Below 60 years: Rs. 2.5 lakh per year
- 60-79 years (Senior Citizens): Rs. 3 lakh per year
- 80+ years (Super Senior Citizens): Rs. 5 lakh per year

Under NEW TAX REGIME (Default from FY 2023-24):
- All ages: Rs. 3 lakh per year
- Tax Rebate u/s 87A: Tax becomes NIL if income up to Rs. 7 lakh

TAX SLABS — NEW REGIME (FY 2024-25, Budget 2024):
- Up to Rs. 3 lakh: NIL
- Rs. 3-7 lakh: 5%
- Rs. 7-10 lakh: 10%
- Rs. 10-12 lakh: 15%
- Rs. 12-15 lakh: 20%
- Above Rs. 15 lakh: 30%
Plus: 4% Health and Education Cess on tax payable.

TAX SLABS — OLD REGIME (FY 2024-25):
- Up to Rs. 2.5 lakh: NIL
- Rs. 2.5-5 lakh: 5%
- Rs. 5-10 lakh: 20%
- Above Rs. 10 lakh: 30%

KEY DEDUCTIONS (AVAILABLE MAINLY UNDER OLD REGIME):
- Section 80C: Up to Rs. 1.5 lakh (PPF, ELSS, LIC, NSC, school fees, home loan principal)
- Section 80D: Medical insurance premium (Rs. 25,000 for self, Rs. 50,000 for senior parents)
- Section 80G: Donations to approved charities
- Section 24(b): Home loan interest up to Rs. 2 lakh
- Section 80E: Interest on education loan (full deduction, no limit)
- Section 80TTA: Interest on savings account up to Rs. 10,000
- Section 80TTB: For senior citizens — interest income up to Rs. 50,000

PART 2: FILING INCOME TAX RETURN (ITR)
========================================

WHO MUST FILE ITR:
1. Anyone with income above the basic exemption limit
2. Anyone who has paid advance tax or has TDS deducted
3. Companies and firms (always must file)
4. NRIs with income from India above threshold
5. Anyone with foreign assets or foreign income

DEADLINE FOR FILING ITR:
- Individuals: July 31 of the assessment year
- Businesses requiring audit: October 31
- Belated return: December 31 (with late fee)

LATE FILING PENALTY:
- Filed after July 31 but before December 31: Rs. 5,000 late fee
- If income below Rs. 5 lakh: Late fee only Rs. 1,000

HOW TO FILE ITR:
1. Online at: www.incometax.gov.in
2. Through CA (Chartered Accountant)
3. At Common Service Centre (CSC)

IMPORTANT ITR FORMS:
- ITR-1 (SAHAJ): For salaried individuals with income up to Rs. 50 lakh
- ITR-2: Salaried + capital gains or foreign income
- ITR-3: Business income
- ITR-4 (SUGAM): Presumptive business income

FORM 26AS AND AIS:
Before filing, check Form 26AS (tax credit statement) and Annual
Information Statement (AIS) at incometax.gov.in — all your income
and TDS deductions are reflected there.

PART 3: TAX DISPUTES AND NOTICES
==================================

TYPES OF INCOME TAX NOTICES:
- Section 143(1): Intimation — usually routine, just requesting documents
- Section 142(1): Enquiry notice — provide information
- Section 143(2): Scrutiny notice — your return is selected for detailed scrutiny
- Section 148: Notice for income escaping assessment
- Section 139: Defective return notice

RESPONDING TO INCOME TAX NOTICES:
1. Read carefully — identify section and what is required
2. DO NOT IGNORE — failure to respond attracts penalties
3. Consult CA if in doubt
4. Respond within given deadline through the e-filing portal
5. Keep copies of all correspondence

INCOME TAX APPEALS:
Level 1: CIT(Appeals) — Commissioner of Income Tax (Appeals)
Level 2: ITAT — Income Tax Appellate Tribunal (factual and legal issues)
Level 3: High Court — Only on substantial questions of law
Level 4: Supreme Court — Further appeal on law

INCOME TAX OMBUDSMAN (DISCONTINUED):
The Income Tax Ombudsman scheme has been discontinued.
Grievances now through: Centralized Processing Cell (CPC)
Helpline: 1800-103-0025 (toll free)
Portal: www.incometaxindiaefiling.gov.in > Grievances

PART 4: TDS (TAX DEDUCTED AT SOURCE)
======================================

WHAT IS TDS:
Tax deducted by the payer before making payment.
Examples: Employer deducts TDS from salary, bank deducts TDS from FD interest.

COMMON TDS RATES:
- Salary (Section 192): As per applicable slab
- Interest (Section 194A): 10%
- Rent above Rs. 50,000/month (Section 194IB): 5%
- Professional fees above Rs. 30,000 (Section 194J): 10%
- Contractor payments (Section 194C): 1-2%

IF EXCESS TDS IS DEDUCTED:
File ITR and claim refund. Refund is credited to your bank account.

PART 5: GST — GOODS AND SERVICES TAX
======================================

GST ACT, 2017:
Replaced all indirect taxes (VAT, Service Tax, Excise Duty etc.)
from July 1, 2017.

GST RATES:
- 0%: Essential items (rice, wheat, milk, books, newspapers, jute, raw foodgrains)
- 5%: Edible oils, sugar, coal, medicines, Life-saving drugs, economy class air travel
- 12%: Processed food, computers, business class travel
- 18%: Most services (restaurant, telecom), electronics, common goods
- 28%: Luxury items (cars, AC, cement, cigarettes, tobacco), plus cess on some

EXEMPTED FROM GST:
- Fresh fruits and vegetables
- Unbranded food grains, flour, curd, lassi
- Eggs, milk, unprocessed meat
- Healthcare services (government hospitals, charitable hospitals)
- Educational services (schools, colleges)
- Agricultural items

CONSUMER'S RIGHT RELATED TO GST:
- You are entitled to a GST bill showing GST amount separately
- MRP (Maximum Retail Price) cannot be exceeded
- Pre-packed goods must show GST-inclusive price

GST ON REAL ESTATE:
- Under-construction properties: 5% GST (no ITC)
- Affordable housing: 1% GST
- Ready-to-move/completed properties: No GST (only stamp duty applies)

GST COMPLAINTS:
1. Anti-Profiteering: If a business doesn't pass GST reduction benefits to consumers.
   Complaint to: Competition Commission of India (CCI) or NAA (National Anti-profiteering Authority)
2. Incorrect billing: Complaint to State GST helpdesk
3. GST Helpline: 1800-103-4786
4. GST Portal: www.gst.gov.in

PART 6: PROPERTY TAX
=====================

PROPERTY TAX is levied by Municipal Corporation/Panchayat.
Based on Annual Rental Value (ARV) or Capital Value of property.

HOW TO PAY:
Pay online or offline to your local Municipal Corporation.
Delhi: mcdonline.gov.in
Mumbai: portal.mcgm.gov.in
Check your city's Municipal Corporation website.

PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS:
- Agricultural land
- Charitable institutions (partial or full)
- Ex-servicemen (in some states)
- Persons below poverty line (BPL) in some states

DISPUTING PROPERTY TAX ASSESSMENT:
1. File objection with Municipal Assessor
2. Appeal to Assessment Appeals Committee
3. Further appeal to Municipal Commissioner
4. Civil Court

PART 7: STAMP DUTY AND REGISTRATION CHARGES
============================================

STAMP DUTY: State subject — varies by state.
Typically:
- Property registration: 3-8% of property value (varies by state)
- Gift deed: Reduced rate in many states (1-3%)
- Will: Nominal stamp duty
- Power of Attorney: Fixed stamp duty

REGISTRATION FEES:
Typically 1% of property value (capped at a maximum amount).

WHERE APPLICABLE:
- Sale deed of immovable property
- Gift deed
- Partition deed
- Lease deed above 11 months
- Development agreement

UNDER-VALUATION OFFENCE:
If property is registered at below Circle Rate (government guideline value),
the registration department can reject or demand stamp duty on circle rate.
